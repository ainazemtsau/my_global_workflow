## Question

What may legitimately exist as shared owner context across processes,
what remains process-local, and how do provenance, relevance, purpose,
sharing, authority, truth, conflict, staleness, promotion, correction,
dispute, revocation, retirement and continuation behave?

## Preserved parent locks

This card does not revise Q1, Q2, Q3 or Q4.

The following accepted positions remain unchanged:

1. Operating-substrate is a stable, model-neutral and carrier-neutral
   kernel with pluggable process packs.

2. Process packs do not modify kernel semantics.

3. Service zones are semantic responsibility families rather than
   physical modules, services, agents, processes, files or deployments.

4. Atomic responsibility claims have one primary normative owner.

5. Owner-profile concerns are genuinely owner-wide preferences,
   policies or approved shared context, not every fact concerning the
   same owner.

6. Capability, permission, authorization, approval and applied effect
   are non-equivalent.

7. Validation, authorization and apply remain distinguishable.

8. Portable continuation is mandatory while its entity model, fields,
   serialization and carrier remain open.

9. Durable mutation crosses a logical validated writer/apply boundary.

10. Q4 staged semantic admission and activation remains accepted.

11. Q4 projects only relevant approved owner-profile inputs under
    provenance, privacy and sharing boundaries.

12. Owner preference cannot weaken kernel semantics or mandatory
    process-pack safety, evidence or effect policy.

13. Changed owner-profile input is an observation, not a hidden mutation
    of an effective process definition.

14. Open obligations receive explicit compatibility, revalidation,
    migration, pin/drain, handoff, rejection or unresolved-owner
    dispositions.

15. No final service-block list or physical topology is accepted.

## Frozen answer

Shared owner context is not:

- one global owner profile;
- one owner-wide truth store;
- one universal transcript or memory dump;
- one automatically inherited configuration layer;
- one permission or authority container.

Shared owner context is a governed semantic boundary through which:

1. an atomic concern becomes eligible for owner-wide availability only
   through legitimate declaration or admission;

2. its normative ownership, provenance, derivation, purpose, sharing
   limits, currentness and known uncertainty remain explicit;

3. one process receives only a minimum relevant and currently permitted
   projection for a bounded purpose;

4. the receiving process separately decides whether the projected
   material is sufficiently reliable, current and applicable to become
   accepted process truth for its obligation;

5. disclosure or use permission remains separate from action authority;

6. promotion, correction, dispute, revocation and retirement cross a
   governed mutation boundary rather than occurring through transcript
   accumulation, repetition, inference or hidden profile edits;

7. changed owner context does not silently rewrite active obligations,
   accepted process state or portable continuations.

The owner/profile responsibility normatively owns owner-wide
declaration, admission and sharing scope.

It does not thereby become normative owner of:

- what an external source asserted;
- the truth of every source claim;
- a process-local accepted conclusion;
- a derived memory's upstream meaning;
- process-pack safety or evidence policy;
- a specific action authorization;
- an already-applied external effect.

## State-model distinctions

These are semantic concern classes. They are not mandatory entities,
schema objects, storage tables, files or a final category catalogue.

### Owner-wide preference or policy

A preference, constraint or policy intentionally declared or explicitly
approved as applicable across more than one process context.

Its primary normative owner is the owner or an explicitly
owner-authorized profile responsibility.

Repeated behavior may support a proposal for such a preference. It does
not create the preference automatically.

An owner-wide preference does not:

- authorize a concrete external effect;
- override a kernel invariant;
- weaken mandatory process-pack policy;
- become accepted solely because a model observed repetition.

### Approved shared context

A source-backed or derived contextual concern admitted for bounded
cross-process availability and projection under a legitimate purpose
and sharing boundary.

Normative ownership remains decomposed:

- the source owns what it asserted;
- the owner/profile responsibility owns owner-wide admission and sharing
  scope;
- the process pack owns local applicability, evidence and safety policy;
- the receiving accepted-state responsibility decides whether the claim
  is accepted for the current obligation.

Approval changes sharing eligibility. It does not turn a source claim
into owner-authored or universally accepted truth.

### Process-local state or assumption

Accepted, provisional, disputed or working material scoped to one
process, obligation, branch or execution context.

This includes, as concern classes:

- process progress and commitments;
- project or canon facts;
- domain-local observations;
- local evidence conclusions;
- temporary routing assumptions;
- one process's interpretation of owner intent;
- one obligation's pending decision or approval.

Default disposition: local.

Concern about the same owner is not sufficient for owner-wide
promotion.

### Source claim

A proposition asserted or observed by a source.

It retains attribution, collection context, time, revision, status,
known limits and relevant provenance.

Origin or integrity verification does not prove that the claim is true,
current enough or sufficient for every receiving process purpose.

### Derived memory

A summary, inference, preference hypothesis, compression, prediction or
other material derived from source claims, process state, history or
interaction.

Derived memory must retain:

- derivation;
- upstream dependencies;
- uncertainty appropriate to the derivation;
- inferred rather than source-asserted status;
- sensitivity to correction, staleness, dispute and revocation of
  upstream material.

Derived memory may be admitted for bounded sharing but remains derived.
Promotion cannot launder inference into source fact.

### Authority or consent

A bounded conclusion permitting disclosure, use, request or action
under a stated purpose, target, scope and conditions.

The following distinctions remain mandatory:

- consent to disclose is not authority to perform an effect;
- standing process permission is not approval of a specific action;
- authorization for one target or payload does not cover a changed
  target or payload;
- one process's consent is not owner-wide consent;
- revocation constrains future use but does not assert that prior lawful
  use or an already-applied effect never occurred.

### Credential or secret

Protected authentication or capability material such as a token, key,
password, recovery material or comparable secret-bearing credential.

Raw secret material does not enter ordinary owner context, memory,
trace, result or portable continuation.

A process may receive a capability or the minimum non-secret facts about
availability, scope and current status. Possession does not create human
authority.

### Accepted process truth

A receiving process's bounded conclusion that a claim is sufficiently
supported, current and applicable for a stated process purpose.

It is distinct from:

- availability in owner context;
- permission to disclose;
- source authenticity;
- owner approval to share;
- technical capability;
- authority to act.

Acceptance in one process does not imply acceptance in another process
with different evidence, freshness, consequence or safety policy.

## Four independent conclusions

For every relevant atomic concern, four conclusions remain independent.

### A. Context availability

May the concern be located or referenced for potential use?

Availability establishes neither disclosure permission, relevance,
truth nor authority.

### B. Permission to disclose or use

May this receiving process obtain or use the concern for this bounded
purpose under current sharing conditions?

Permission may allow only a subset, abstraction, protected reference or
limited use.

### C. Authority to act

May the current identity perform or approve this concrete action
against this target under current policy?

Descriptive context, preference, technical capability, credential
possession or prior consent does not answer this automatically.

### D. Accepted process truth

Does the process accept the claim as sufficiently reliable, current and
applicable for the present obligation?

Source verification, owner-wide admission and permission to disclose
are inputs to this conclusion, not substitutes for it.

No one of these four conclusions is inferred automatically from another.

## Legitimate owner-wide concerns

A concern may legitimately become owner-wide through one of these
routes:

1. The owner explicitly declares a low-risk preference or policy as
   cross-process.

2. The owner or an owner-authorized responsibility admits a
   source-backed contextual claim for stated cross-process purposes.

3. A previously accepted standing owner policy legitimately covers the
   narrow admission class.

4. Derived memory is admitted for bounded sharing while retaining its
   derived status, uncertainty and upstream lineage.

For a low-risk directly owner-declared preference, declaration, scope
check, conflict check and governed apply may form one compact bounded
transition.

Evidence and procedural burden are consequence-sensitive. Q5 does not
require high-assurance ceremony for every language, presentation or
similarly low-risk preference.

This compact path does not permit transcript inference, repeated
behavior or hidden mutation to become owner-wide truth.

## Local by default

The following remain process-local by default:

- process progress and open obligations;
- commitments and branch-local decisions;
- domain-local observations and working hypotheses;
- local canon, project facts and project rules;
- local evidence conclusions;
- temporary routing assumptions;
- one process's interpretation of owner intent or taste;
- consent, approval or authority for one bounded disclosure or effect;
- pending decisions and authorizations;
- transcript fragments;
- automatically ingested memory;
- model-generated preference hypotheses;
- disputed or stale material;
- raw credentials and secrets.

This is not a final content catalogue.

The governing rule is:

process-local material remains local unless a separate legitimate
promotion transition establishes owner-wide eligibility.

## Promotion

Promotion is a governed durable change in cross-process availability
and projection scope.

Promotion does not:

- prove truth;
- transfer source ownership;
- convert derived memory into source fact;
- grant disclosure for an unrelated purpose;
- grant effect authority;
- override process-pack policy;
- establish local acceptance in every receiving process.

### Promotion requirements

Promotion requires, as applicable:

1. Atomic concern identification.

   The candidate is narrow enough for source, normative owner, purpose,
   conflicts and status to be evaluated independently.

2. Legitimate owner-wide intent.

   At least one holds:

   - explicit owner declaration;
   - explicit owner or owner-authorized admission;
   - an accepted standing policy that legitimately covers the case.

   Repetition is evidence for a proposal, not automatic promotion.

3. Cross-process relevance.

   The concern has explicit owner-wide applicability or demonstrated
   legitimate use across materially different process pressures,
   followed by owner-authorized admission.

4. Provenance and derivation.

   The boundary retains who asserted or derived the concern, collection
   context, time, transformations, uncertainty and current status.

5. Purpose and eligible consumers.

   The cross-process purpose is bounded enough to reject unrelated
   secondary use.

6. Sharing and privacy legitimacy.

   Projection does not violate applicable owner, subject, third-party or
   process sharing boundaries.

7. Minimization.

   Only the minimum useful claim, abstraction or reference is promoted.

8. Currentness interpretation.

   The receiving process can determine whether use-time, periodic or
   event-triggered revalidation is required.

9. Conflict assessment.

   Promotion does not override kernel semantics, weaken mandatory
   process policy, duplicate normative ownership, expand effect
   authority or turn capability metadata into permission.

10. Correction, dispute, revocation and retirement route.

    Future projection can be constrained or stopped, and dependent work
    can be reconciled without rewriting history.

11. Governed apply.

    The legitimate owner of the owner-wide boundary validates and
    applies the transition and returns evidence of its disposition.

12. Dependent-work treatment.

    Active obligations and continuations that rely or may rely on the
    concern receive explicit compatibility or revalidation
    dispositions.

### Promotion blockers

Promotion is blocked or remains unresolved when:

- the candidate is only a model inference, transcript fragment,
  temporary assumption or unreviewed summary;
- provenance or derivation is materially missing or ambiguous;
- the asserted normative owner is illegitimate;
- no owner-wide purpose or approved sharing scope exists;
- the sole rationale is that the same owner uses both processes;
- the concern is stale for its consequence and cannot be revalidated;
- a material dispute prevents safe reliance;
- source validity, disclosure permission or applicable authority has
  been revoked;
- the concern conflicts with kernel or mandatory process policy;
- promotion would broaden action authority or consent;
- the proposed projection is broader than necessary;
- raw secret-bearing credential material is included;
- sensitive local material is proposed for unrelated secondary use;
- source identity would be erased;
- derived material would be relabelled as source fact;
- no correction, restriction or retirement route exists;
- mutation would occur through hidden profile editing;
- dependent active work cannot be reconciled or assigned a resolution
  owner.

A blocked concern may remain:

- process-local evidence;
- a source claim;
- derived memory;
- a bounded proposal;
- an explicit unresolved obligation with a current resolution owner.

## Projection

Promotion, projection and local acceptance are separate.

- Promotion establishes eligibility for bounded owner-wide availability.
- Projection supplies a relevant and permitted subset to one process.
- Local acceptance determines whether that process may rely on it.

Projection may occur on demand or during Q4 process admission.

A legitimate projection establishes semantically:

- bounded process purpose;
- relevance to the current obligation;
- minimum supplied material or reference;
- source and derivation provenance;
- applicable sharing and privacy conditions;
- currentness and known status limitations;
- whether use-time revalidation is required;
- the concern kind: preference, policy, source-backed context, derived
  material, authority evidence or capability metadata;
- conflicts with process-owned policy;
- the unavailable, rejected or degraded behavior.

Projection must not:

- supply the complete profile by default;
- copy unrelated process history;
- expose raw credentials;
- infer authority from access;
- force local process acceptance;
- weaken a mandatory process rule;
- hide whether the material is source-backed, inferred, disputed,
  stale or restricted.

## Provenance, purpose, privacy and currentness

### Provenance

The receiving process must be able to determine:

- who asserted or declared the concern;
- whether it was directly supplied, observed, inferred or derived;
- the originating process or collection context;
- supporting evidence;
- transformations applied;
- corrected, superseded or invalidated predecessors;
- remaining uncertainty.

Owner confirmation and model inference are different claims with
different normative owners.

### Relevance

Relevance is evaluated for the receiving process and present obligation.

A genuinely owner-wide concern may still be irrelevant to one process.

Technical access does not establish relevance.

### Purpose limitation

Approval for one purpose does not permit unrelated reuse.

A materially new purpose requires a new legitimate projection or
authority disposition.

### Least privilege and minimization

Projection prefers:

- a narrow claim over a complete record;
- an abstract constraint over sensitive detail when sufficient;
- a validated accessible reference over unnecessary duplication;
- capability use over disclosure of secret material.

### Privacy and sharing

Sharing boundaries apply to:

- claim content;
- metadata and provenance;
- sensitive source identity;
- derived memory;
- access logs;
- status checks;
- continuation content;
- downstream redistribution.

The owner cannot authorize disclosure of third-party material where the
owner is not the legitimate authority.

### Currentness

No universal time-to-live is accepted.

Currentness depends on:

- claim type;
- source behavior;
- purpose;
- consequence of error;
- known change triggers;
- process-pack policy.

Higher-consequence or rapidly changing claims generally require
stronger use-time evidence than durable presentation preferences.

## Change semantics

### Staleness

A stale concern requires:

- revalidation from an acceptable source;
- a process-pack-declared safe degraded route;
- or block/wait/unresolved treatment with a named owner.

Cached owner context is not a substitute for currentness evidence.

### Correction

Correction:

- establishes the currently accepted replacement through governed apply;
- preserves relation to the prior claim;
- records who corrected what and why;
- identifies affected projections and active work;
- triggers bounded revalidation where reliance may change.

Correction is not silent overwrite.

### Dispute

Dispute:

- preserves competing claims and provenance;
- prohibits generic last-write-wins;
- names a current resolution owner;
- restricts high-consequence reliance where uncertainty is material;
- permits provisional or degraded use only where process-pack policy
  explicitly makes it safe.

### Revocation

Revocation may concern:

- permission to disclose;
- permission to use;
- action authority;
- source or credential validity;
- technical capability access.

Revocation may prohibit future use, invalidate pending authorization or
require reconciliation.

Revocation does not:

- automatically make an underlying descriptive claim false;
- erase prior lawful use;
- erase an already-applied effect;
- constitute rollback.

An already-applied effect may require a new governed compensation path.

### Retirement

Retirement is governed closure, not simple deletion.

A retired concern no longer enters new projections unless separately
re-admitted.

Retirement may be blocked while:

- active obligations depend on the concern;
- prior work cannot be interpreted without its provenance;
- authority or effect status remains unresolved;
- required evidence or audit would be destroyed;
- correction or dispute lineage would become unintelligible.

Historical evidence may remain while permission for new use ends.

### Inaccessibility

Inaccessible is distinct from false, revoked, retired and nonexistent.

A declared degraded route is valid only where the concern is genuinely
optional and all remaining kernel, safety, authority, result and
continuation obligations hold.

Otherwise the process waits, blocks, migrates, hands off or returns an
unresolved disposition. Fabrication is prohibited.

## Conflict rules

Q2/Q4 atomic normative ownership remains authoritative.

### Owner preference versus kernel invariant

The preference is rejected as inapplicable to the conflicting claim.

Owner preference cannot legalize kernel override.

### Owner preference versus mandatory process safety or effect policy

Mandatory process policy constrains the preference.

The owner receives only decisions that the policy legitimately leaves
open.

Owner preference is not a bypass.

### Shared claim versus process-local accepted state

Shared context does not silently overwrite process state.

The process validates provenance, currentness and applicability, then
uses explicit compatible-resume, revalidation, migration, pin/drain or
unresolved treatment.

### Conflicting source claims

Both claims and their provenance are preserved.

The receiving process applies its evidence and consequence policy.

Arrival order and profile insertion order do not determine truth.

### Sharing approval versus action authority

Sharing approval permits only bounded disclosure or use.

Any effect follows a separate propose, validate, decide/authorize and
apply path.

### Technical capability versus permission

Capability may be reported as available.

Use remains bounded by permission, authority and process policy.

### Revoked permission versus pinned work

Pinning may preserve interpretation, history and evidence.

Pinning cannot preserve revoked disclosure permission, use permission or
action authority.

Affected work must restrict, revalidate, migrate, reauthorize, stop or
return unresolved.

Generic last-write-wins, inherited-profile override and automatic
process-local write-back are prohibited.

## Active obligations and portable continuation

Changed owner context is an observation.

It does not silently mutate the effective process definition or every
active obligation.

Each affected obligation receives one explicit disposition:

1. compatible resume;
2. bounded revalidation or migration;
3. pin and drain;
4. accepted handoff;
5. block or rejection;
6. unresolved with a named current resolution owner.

### Pinning rule

Pinning may preserve:

- interpretation;
- prior evidence;
- definition compatibility;
- historical reasoning basis.

Pinning does not preserve:

- revoked disclosure permission;
- revoked use permission;
- expired or revoked action authority;
- permission now conflicting with mandatory policy.

### Q5 continuation semantic minimum

Without defining exact fields, portable continuation must preserve
enough meaning to determine:

- which obligation depends on owner-wide or local context;
- whether relied material was source-backed, owner-declared, derived,
  provisional, disputed or locally accepted;
- provenance and currentness/status limitations;
- purpose and sharing boundaries;
- context availability separately from action authority;
- compatibility with changed context or process definition;
- whether revalidation, migration, restriction, pin/drain, handoff or
  closure is required;
- current next-action and resolution owners;
- next legitimate action.

Continuation must not:

- embed raw secrets;
- copy sensitive material merely for convenience;
- use an inaccessible opaque reference without declaring the access
  requirement and failure route;
- silently authorize renewed use;
- omit known dispute, restriction, revocation or staleness.

Exact continuation entities, fields and serialization remain Q9/Q12.

## Health-like privacy and safety pressure

This is a semantic boundary test, not actual Health policy.

Legitimate owner-wide concerns may include:

- explicitly cross-process language or accessibility preference;
- approved privacy and sharing defaults;
- owner-wide decision-routing preferences within mandatory domain
  constraints;
- another approved shared constraint with established purpose and
  provenance.

Local by default:

- domain observations and hypotheses;
- measurements and symptoms;
- diagnoses or treatment facts;
- risk estimates;
- local evidence conclusions;
- clinician, model or process summaries;
- local consent or approval;
- disputed or stale health-related claims.

Boundary behavior:

- a health-related source claim requires explicit bounded admission for
  cross-process projection;
- permission to share does not prove clinical truth;
- another process does not receive the claim merely because it concerns
  the same owner;
- mandatory evidence, safety and effect policy remains process-pack
  owned;
- owner preference cannot lower mandatory requirements;
- stale, disputed, inaccessible or revoked mandatory context blocks
  risky reliance unless a safe declared degraded route exists;
- continuation minimizes sensitive content and preserves provenance,
  access and revalidation requirements.

Verdict: PASS.

The boundary supports strict privacy, provenance and fail-closed
behavior without making Health policy universal or copying sensitive
context into unrelated processes.

## Game/productive-play creative pressure

This is a semantic boundary test, not an actual game process pack.

Legitimate owner-wide concerns may include:

- explicitly cross-process language or feedback preferences;
- declared initiative and steering preferences;
- approved privacy defaults;
- a broad creative preference explicitly intended to span projects.

Local by default:

- project canon;
- project goals and rules;
- branch-specific ideas;
- playtest findings;
- temporary aesthetic hypotheses;
- one project's interpretation of owner taste;
- local approval to publish or mutate a project artifact.

Boundary behavior:

- repeated preference may create a proposal but not a hidden owner-wide
  update;
- project canon never becomes owner-profile truth;
- a lower-stakes process may define a useful degraded route when
  optional owner context is stale or unavailable;
- uncertainty and changed result quality remain visible;
- irreversible publication still requires separate authority and apply;
- Health-specific policy is not imported.

Verdict: PASS.

The same boundary permits flexible creative work without turning local
project memory into global authority.

## Rejected alternatives

1. Inherited owner baseline with process-local overlays as the
   architecture backbone.

   Rejected because baseline inheritance encourages global dumping,
   generic precedence, accidental authority and attempts to weaken
   mandatory process policy.

   Only the descriptive view of normative layers is retained.

2. Pure on-demand disclosure as the complete architecture.

   Rejected because it does not by itself define admission, promotion,
   correction, dispute, retirement or active-work lifecycle, and live
   source dependence can make continuation fragile.

   Purpose-bound on-demand disclosure is retained as a subordinate
   projection pattern.

3. Global owner profile or owner-wide truth store.

   Rejected because it merges source claims, accepted truth, sharing
   permission and authority.

4. Automatic transcript or memory promotion.

   Rejected because occurrence, repetition, retrieval and inference do
   not establish owner-wide normative ownership.

5. Generic last-write-wins.

   Rejected because arrival or insertion order cannot determine truth,
   authority, state ownership or mandatory policy.

6. Owner profile overriding mandatory policy.

   Rejected because owner preference is an input rather than a bypass
   around kernel or process-pack guardrails.

7. Revocation or retirement as deletion or rollback.

   Rejected because future-use prohibition does not erase provenance,
   lawful prior use or already-applied effects.

8. Raw credentials as ordinary shared context.

   Rejected because protected capability material requires a separate
   boundary.

9. Profile completeness as evidence of usefulness.

   Rejected because comprehensive internal context surfaces can still
   create owner-facing bureaucracy and ineffective operation.

## Required failure defenses

The architecture explicitly defends against:

- global context dumping through minimum purpose-scoped projection;
- accidental cross-process authority through four independent
  conclusions;
- stale assumptions through currentness and use-time revalidation;
- secret leakage through a protected capability boundary;
- automatic local-to-global promotion through governed admission/apply;
- hidden profile mutation through explicit proposal, validation,
  authority where applicable, apply and result;
- derived-memory laundering through retained derivation and uncertainty;
- availability/permission conflation through consumer- and
  purpose-specific use decisions;
- consent/action conflation through a separate effect path;
- last-write-wins conflict through normative ownership and explicit
  resolution;
- profile override of mandatory policy through non-overridable
  guardrails;
- continuation leakage through semantic dependencies and protected
  references;
- retirement-as-deletion through lineage and dependency treatment;
- inaccessible references reported as resumable through explicit access
  and failure semantics;
- revocation reported as rollback through truthful historical and effect
  semantics;
- status-correlation leakage by including status checks in privacy and
  minimization analysis;
- profile-completeness bureaucracy through consequence-sensitive
  admission and live-use usefulness pressure.
