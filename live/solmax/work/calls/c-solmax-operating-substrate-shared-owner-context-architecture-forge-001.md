  Q5 research findings:
  - Shared owner context is best treated as a governed admission and
    projection boundary, not a global profile dump.
  - Owner-wide preference/policy, approved shared context, process-local
    state/assumption, source claim, derived memory, authority/consent and
    credential/secret are required semantic distinctions but not frozen
    entities or schema.
  - The owner/profile responsibility can own owner-wide declaration,
    admission and sharing scope without becoming normative owner of every
    source claim's truth.
  - Context availability, disclosure/use permission, action authority and
    accepted process truth are independent conclusions.
  - Source verification establishes origin/integrity/current status, not
    truth or fitness for every process purpose.
  - Promotion changes eligibility for cross-process projection. It does not
    transfer source ownership, prove truth or grant effect authority.
  - Promotion needs legitimate owner-wide intent, provenance,
    cross-process relevance, purpose limitation, minimization, privacy/
    sharing legitimacy, currentness, conflict checks, governed apply and
    correction/revocation/retirement behavior.
  - Transcript occurrence, repeated behavior, local acceptance, model
    inference, stale/disputed input, missing provenance, secret material,
    policy conflict and hidden mutation block automatic promotion.
  - Purpose-scoped least-privilege projection is preferred to full-profile
    replication.
  - Correction, dispute, revocation and retirement are non-equivalent.
  - Revocation constrains future use or authority but does not pretend prior
    lawful use or already-applied effects never occurred.
  - Pinning can preserve interpretation and evidence, but cannot preserve
    revoked disclosure permission or action authority.
  - Changed context gives every affected obligation a compatible-resume,
    revalidation/migration, pin/drain, handoff, block/rejection or named
    unresolved-owner disposition.
  - Portable continuation preserves context dependency, provenance,
    currentness, sharing, authority, compatibility and next route without
    requiring raw sensitive content or secrets.
  - Health-like and game/productive-play pressures pass without copying
    sensitive/local context or domain policy between processes.
  - Failure modes include global dumping, accidental cross-process
    authority, stale assumptions, secret leakage, automatic promotion,
    hidden mutation, derived-memory laundering, last-write-wins, status
    correlation, continuation leakage and profile-completeness bureaucracy.
  - Candidate directions:
    A. governed owner-context admission and scoped projection;
    B. inherited baseline with process-local overlays;
    C. on-demand purpose-bound context disclosure.
  - Research recommendation: A as the backbone, C as its subordinate access
    pattern, and no generic overlay precedence from B.
  - hidden_prerequisite: none.
  - gap_event: none.
