  Q6 plain question:
  "Какие persistence/history responsibilities are substrate-level, and
  what remains consumer-specific?"

  Why it matters:
  Durable continuity is central to stateful processes, but persistence
  and history can mean current accepted state, event or transition
  history, audit, evidence ledger, decision history, source provenance,
  recovery checkpoint, definition/version history, context summary or
  retention state. These must be separated semantically without copying
  Direction OS or Zaratusta file surfaces.

  Accepted parent locks:
  - Q1-Q5 remain unchanged.
  - Service zones are semantic responsibility families, not physical
    services or storage blocks.
  - Atomic claims have one primary normative owner.
  - One primary normative state owner establishes each accepted durable
    version within a mutable-state boundary.
  - Query, evidence, proposal, validation, authorization, apply, result
    and continuation remain distinct.
  - A result or artifact does not automatically establish accepted
    durable truth.
  - Durable mutation crosses a logical validated apply boundary.
  - Portable continuation preserves legitimate resume semantics.
  - Process-pack instantiation uses staged admission and activation.
  - Owner-wide admission, sharing eligibility, process-local truth and
    action authority remain distinct.
  - Corrected, disputed, revoked and retired material requires lineage
    and active-work treatment.
  - No final service-block list, storage system, schema, repository,
    carrier or implementation is accepted.

  Research should compare current primary sources from at least:
  - provenance and lineage standards;
  - durable workflow/checkpoint/recovery systems;
  - audit, event or state-transition models;
  - long-lived definition/version compatibility;
  - privacy, correction, restriction and retention governance where
    relevant.

  Current artifacts are evidence, not authority.
