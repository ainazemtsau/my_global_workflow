  3. COMPLETE CANDIDATE PACK SEMANTIC OUTLINE
  ===========================================================================

  Working name:

    Evidence-to-Decision

  Status:

  The organization below is an exemplar-specific semantic outline, not a

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.1 Objective
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Turn one owner decision question into a bounded comparison of two or three
  legitimate options and return:

  - one concise recommendation or bounded owner decision;
  - material supporting and opposing evidence;
  - material uncertainties;
  - unresolved obligations and current owners;
  - a portable continuation.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.2 Applicability
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Applicable when:

  - the requested outcome is advice or decision support;
  - at least two legitimate options can be established;
  - mandatory criteria and source obligations can be established;
  - the process remains read-only;
  - a recommendation, bounded question or explicit block is meaningful.

  Not applicable when:

  - the owner asks the process to perform or commit the selected action;
  - the request inherently requires a durable or external effect;
  - no legitimate comparison set can be established;
  - an emergency action rather than advisory analysis is required;
  - mandatory domain policy requires a different qualified process;
  - mandatory criteria or evidence are unavailable and no safe degraded
    route exists.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.3 Concrete process boundaries
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  In scope:

  - frame the decision question;
  - establish legitimate options;
  - discover decision criteria;
  - determine mandatory versus optional criteria;
  - establish source and evidence requirements;
  - collect or request permitted evidence;
  - evaluate provenance, currentness, applicability and limitations;
  - compare options;
  - expose material trade-offs;
  - form a recommendation or bounded owner decision;
  - return result and continuation.

  Out of scope:

  - commit the owner to an option;
  - alter authoritative project or process state;
  - purchase, send, publish, delete, sign or contact third parties;
  - grant authority or permission;
  - invent criteria, values, options, sources or evidence;
  - silently promote provisional material into accepted truth;
  - silently create a new domain procedure;
  - treat recommendation as authorization or applied effect.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.4 Process vocabulary
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  decision question:
    The bounded choice the owner wants resolved.

  option:
    A candidate course or answer considered by the process.

  legitimate option:
    An option satisfying known non-negotiable eligibility conditions.

  criterion:
    A dimension used to evaluate options.

  mandatory criterion:
    A criterion whose absence blocks legitimate completion because it may
    disqualify an option, represents a binding constraint, or can reverse
    the recommendation.

  criterion value:
    The accepted or explicitly provisional value used for this obligation.

  governing source:
    A source whose authority or declared role makes it relevant to a
    mandatory constraint or claim.

  source requirement:
    The process-owned rule stating what source class is required for a claim
    or criterion.

  evidence:
    Material supporting, refuting or qualifying a claim, criterion value,
    comparison or recommendation.

  evidence sufficiency:
    The pack-owned conclusion that evidence is adequate for the present
    process purpose and consequence.

  uncertainty:
    A known limitation, conflict, range, staleness or missing information.

  unresolved obligation:
    A bounded unresolved item with one current next-action owner.

  decision brief:
    The concise owner-facing artifact containing comparison material.

  result:
    The achieved, blocked, unresolved or checkpoint disposition settling
    the bounded obligation.

  portable continuation:
    Enough accepted semantics for a fresh legitimate run to resume without
    reconstructing the prior transcript.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.5 Local procedure catalogue
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  These are local candidate procedures, not a universal substrate catalogue.

  P1 — frame-decision

    Establish the decision question, scope, horizon, known owner constraints,
    advisory boundary and expected closure.

  P2 — establish-legitimate-options

    Establish two or three options and test them against known eligibility and
    disqualification conditions.

  P3 — establish-criteria-and-source-obligations

    Derive candidate criteria, classify mandatory criteria and assign source
    requirements.

  P4 — acquire-and-qualify-evidence

    Obtain permitted source material and preserve provenance, currentness,
    limitations and conflict status.

  P5 — compare-options

    Evaluate every legitimate option against every mandatory criterion and
    declared optional criteria.

  P6 — form-recommendation

    Determine whether one option is supportably preferable, whether the
    remaining trade-off belongs to the owner, or whether completion is
    blocked.

  P7 — return-decision-brief

    Integrate recommendation, evidence, uncertainty, unresolved owners,
    result disposition and continuation.

  P8 — resolve-missing-or-conflicting-input

    Ask one bounded clarification, query an authoritative source, return a
    clear block, or preserve an unresolved continuation.

  One implementation may physically combine these procedures. Their semantic
  obligations remain independently inspectable and testable.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.6 Criterion and source policy
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Candidate criterion inputs:

  - explicit owner constraints in the intake;
  - option eligibility and disqualification conditions;
  - governing-source requirements;
  - applicable process-local dimensions such as benefit/fit, feasibility,
    cost/resources, risk/downside, time horizon, reversibility and
    dependencies;
  - domain criteria legitimately required by the decision.

  A criterion is mandatory when at least one holds:

  - it may disqualify an option;
  - it expresses a binding owner or governing-source constraint;
  - its value could reverse the recommendation;
  - omission would make the recommendation materially misleading;
  - applicable pack policy makes it mandatory for the consequence class.

  Before recommendation, the process asks internally:

    Is there a plausible missing criterion, source requirement or unresolved
    value whose legitimate value could change option eligibility or reverse
    the recommendation?

  A positive answer prevents a completed recommendation.

  Source rules:

  - owner-specific preference or threshold:
    obtain from the owner for this bounded obligation;
  - external factual claim:
    use a current authoritative or primary source appropriate to consequence;
  - option/provider claim:
    prefer the provider's official primary source and disclose self-interest;
  - independently verifiable claim:
    use an independent primary or high-quality source when required;
  - derived estimate:
    preserve method, inputs, assumptions, range and provenance;
  - conflicting sources:
    preserve both, apply declared evidence policy and route an unresolved
    material difference rather than using arrival order.

  Authenticity does not automatically establish truth, applicability or
  evidence sufficiency.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.7 Local routing policy
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Route A — complete and resolvable intake:
    Run P1-P7 and return a decision brief.

  Route B — one material ambiguity:
    Ask the minimum bounded question that changes the next legitimate route.

  Route C — mandatory input missing:
    Ask for the value or an authoritative source; otherwise return blocked or
    unresolved.

  Route D — conflicting legitimate policies or preferences:
    Route only the material decision to the appropriate authority owner.

  Route E — no legitimate comparison set:
    Return a clear no-applicable-comparison result with known facts and
    recovery route.

  Route F — mandatory source or capability unavailable:
    Use a declared semantically valid degraded route or block. Never silently
    substitute a different capability or stale memory.

  Route G — optional capability unavailable:
    Use the declared degraded route and disclose the changed presentation,
    coverage or confidence.

  Route H — durable or external effect requested:
    Refuse to treat recommendation as execution; return a bounded proposal or
    handoff requirement for a separate effect-capable process.

  Route I — definition or compatibility conflict:
    Revalidate, pin, migrate, hand off, block or retain a named unresolved
    obligation. Never reinterpret active work silently.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.8 State and information distinctions
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  governing process definition:
    Accepted semantics interpreting the work. Candidate existence does not
    establish activation.

  current run context:
    Present question, provisional options, working criteria, current procedure
    and temporary reasoning material.

  accepted basis:
    Current accepted owner inputs, governing-source conclusions and
    definition references used for this obligation.

  accepted process state:
    A bounded accepted conclusion established by its legitimate state owner.
    Normal runtime of this read-only pack creates no durable accepted state.

  source:
    Originating actor, material, observation or system asserting or supplying
    a claim.

  memory:
    Retrieved or derived material that may assist reasoning. Retrieval does
    not establish accepted truth.

  history:
    Relevant prior occurrences, routing, evidence, corrections and results.
    History does not independently determine current truth or authority.

  evidence:
    Material supporting, refuting or qualifying a claim, with provenance and
    known limitations.

  artifact:
    A separately addressable output such as the decision brief. Existence does
    not prove correctness, closure or authoritative mutation.

  result:
    The disposition settling or checkpointing the present obligation and
    naming remaining owners.

  portable continuation:
    The semantic recovery contract for legitimate resume. It is not a
    transcript dump or opaque runtime cursor.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.9 Comparison and recommendation policy
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Compare only legitimate options.
  - Evaluate every option against every mandatory criterion.
  - Do not hide missing mandatory evidence inside an aggregate score.
  - Treat scoring as optional representation, not authority.
  - Preserve material qualitative trade-offs.
  - Distinguish evidence from interpretation.
  - Expose sensitivity to uncertain values.
  - Do not recommend while a recommendation-reversing criterion remains
    unresolved.
  - When several legitimate trade-offs depend on owner preference, return a
    bounded owner decision rather than choosing arbitrarily.
  - Recommendation remains advisory and is neither authorization nor apply.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.10 Authority, effects and escalation
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Owner-facing integration owner:

    Owns the current obligation, internal integration, clarification
    monitoring, closure and continuation.

  Human authority owner:

    Owns decision-specific subjective thresholds, preference trade-offs and
    any actual commitment to an option.

  Source responsibility:

    Owns what its source asserted and the stated provenance of that assertion.

  Evidence evaluator:

    The Evidence-to-Decision pack owns whether supplied evidence is sufficient,
    current and applicable for this process purpose.

  Operational authority:

    Read-only query, evidence evaluation, comparison and advisory return.

  External or durable effect authority:

    none

  Escalation:

  - missing subjective value -> owner;
  - missing factual value -> acceptable authoritative source;
  - disputed mandatory evidence -> named evidence-resolution owner;
  - domain policy outside competence -> qualified process or handoff;
  - requested effect -> separate proposal and validated effect route;
  - unresolved ownership -> named resolution owner, never ownerless waiting.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.11 Writer policy
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Normal runtime writer invocation:

    none

  Rules:

  - reasoning output does not become authoritative durable state;
  - the decision brief is result material, not an applied decision;
  - no repository, profile, project or external system is mutated;
  - save, publish, send or execute requests create a separate bounded effect
    proposal outside this process;
  - any future effect path must establish fresh accepted basis, validation,

  3.12 Capability requirements and degraded routes
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Mandatory semantic capabilities:

  - receive an ordinary owner decision question;
  - ask one bounded clarification or return a block;
  - resolve or request mandatory source material;
  - preserve source provenance and known limitations;
  - evaluate evidence currentness and applicability;
  - compare options against declared criteria;
  - detect unresolved recommendation-reversing criteria;
  - form an evidence-bounded recommendation or owner decision;
  - name unresolved owners;
  - produce portable continuation.

  Optional semantic capabilities:

  - automatic external search;
  - parallel research;
  - structured scoring;
  - tabular rendering;
  - visualization;
  - formatted citations;
  - persistent history materialization;
  - automated continuation transport.

  - no parallelism:
    execute the same obligations sequentially;
  - no structured scoring:

  - no persistent continuation store:
    return self-contained inline continuation;
  - no optional contextual source:

  No legitimate degraded route exists for:

  - a missing recommendation-reversing criterion;
  - missing mandatory evidence;
  - fabricated provenance;
  - insufficient authority;
  - an external effect in this read-only process;
  - continuation that loses unresolved ownership.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  3.13 Success, stop, failure and recovery
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Success:

  - two or three legitimate options established;
  - every mandatory criterion established;
  - mandatory evidence requirements satisfied;
  - material conflicts and uncertainty visible;
  - recommendation follows from the declared comparison;
  - remaining non-blocking obligations have owners;
  - decision brief and continuation returned.

  Bounded stop:

  - exactly one material owner value or source decision is required;
  - the question states why it matters and what it blocks;
  - completed useful work is preserved without false completion.

  Blocked result:

  - a mandatory criterion, source, capability or authority is unavailable;
  - the blocking fact, resolution owner and recovery route are explicit.

  Failure-known-not-done:

  - evidence acquisition or comparison failed before a valid recommendation;
  - no recommendation is reported.

  Partial result:

  - useful facts or option analysis exist, but mandatory closure conditions
    do not;
  - partial material is not presented as the completed decision outcome.

  Outcome unknown:

  - not normally applicable to external effects because the pack performs no
    external effect;
  - unknown child/source outcome still returns unresolved rather than
    fabricated completion.

  Recovery:

  - supply the missing criterion value;
  - restore or provide an acceptable mandatory source;
  - correct a source claim and revalidate affected comparisons;
  - resolve a source conflict;
  - bind a missing mandatory capability;
  - resume from continuation;
  - hand off to a qualified process;
  - explicitly close unresolved when no legitimate route remains.

  Recovery does not erase prior evidence or silently rewrite history.
