  ## Proof required before useful live-process claims

  1. Structural-admission claim
     Before saying a pack is admissible, evidence must show that its effective
     definition preserves Q1-Q7, declares process-owned policy, resolves or
     explicitly degrades capabilities, names ownership and authority, and
     supplies result/continuation obligations. This is conformance evidence,
     not live-use evidence.

  2. Useful live-behavior claim
     At least one real owner-originated obligation must run through the actual
     pack end-to-end and return a useful result, bounded decision, clear
     block/failure or continuation. The owner must not need to read or operate
     internal routing, audit, state or lifecycle machinery to obtain the
     useful consequence.

  3. Portable-continuity claim
     A fresh run must resume from the produced continuation without relying on
     the prior transcript or the owner's reconstruction from memory. It must
     correctly preserve governing definition, accepted basis, open
     obligations, owners, authority, effect certainty and next legitimate
     action.

  4. Failure/no-route claim
     At least one consequence-relevant adverse branch must be exercised, such
     as ambiguity, missing mandatory input, no applicable procedure, missing
     mandatory capability, interruption, cancellation or inaccessible source.
     The process must avoid fabricated input and silent fallback, retain a
     current resolution owner and produce useful bounded closure.

  5. Effect-safety claim
     Required only when the pack claims durable or external effects. Evidence
     must distinguish proposal, validation, authorization, apply and result;
     show an authoritative apply receipt or explicit non-success disposition;
     and demonstrate that partial or outcome-unknown effects do not cause
     false success or blind retry. A read-only process does not silently pass
     this claim; the claim is simply out of scope.

  6. Governed-evolution claim
     Required before saying procedure or pack evolution works in practice.
     A changed definition affecting active work must produce an explicit
     compatible-resume, revalidation/migration, pin/drain, handoff,
     block/rejection or named-unresolved disposition without retrospective
     reinterpretation.

  7. Reusable-substrate claim
     Required before claiming the substrate has demonstrated cross-process
     reuse. At least two materially different concrete process packs must
     preserve the same kernel semantics without kernel edits or consumer
     leakage, while retaining materially different pack-owned procedures,
     policies, evidence thresholds and interaction rhythms.

  These are minimum proof categories, not a detailed test plan, benchmark,
  numerical quality threshold or selected proof harness.
