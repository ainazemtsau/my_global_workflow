### A. Domain authority and planning

- **W1 — Thin training domain**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Training/activity attaches as a namespaced thin domain and contributes only domain data, procedure definitions, one state-machine instance, one cursor instance, and one registry line; it contributes no router, lifecycle, gate, writer barrier, scheduler, server, database, or background worker. →I1/I2
  - acceptance: A verifier can remove every training file and registry entry without finding training semantics embedded in kernel or nutrition files. →I1/I7

- **W2 — Canonical authority**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Health AI holds the ACTIVE training program, bounded planning layers, progression/regression rules, safety boundaries, session prescriptions, normalized session LOGs, reviews, and scoped mutations. →G-TA-1/S1
  - acceptance: A fresh provider can identify the current authoritative plan, today’s applicable prescription, recent normalized outcomes, and next review state from committed files alone. →S6/S10/G-TA-1

- **W3 — Personalized setup and system-decided programming**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Setup reuses current shared owner facts and asks only missing irreducible facts that materially affect safety or feasibility; exercise selection, split, volume, intensity, progression, conditioning mix, and deload logic are system-decided from evidence, current phase, constraints, and feedback. →S2/S5/S12
  - acceptance: Setup does not ask the owner to design expert variables, does not fabricate missing personal facts, and produces a complete proposal for owner approval. →S2/S10/S11

- **W4 — Session brief / training today**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: From ACTIVE authority and a natural request such as home, gym, morning, bicycle, VR, or “what is today,” the system produces a readable session brief with purpose, ordered work, planned effort, warm-up, permitted substitutions, technique/safety cues, and the result signals needed afterward. →G-TA-2/S1/S3
  - acceptance: The brief is executable without reading internal files and refuses or safely substitutes a request that conflicts with ACTIVE authority or a safety brake. →S2/G-TA-2

### B. Strength, activity, adherence, and safety

- **W5 — Strength and body-composition contour**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: The strength contour maintains a reproducible non-maximal baseline, progressive or regressive rules, technique constraints, recovery checks, and a response to sustained strength or wellbeing decline during weight loss. →S1/S2
  - acceptance: Review can distinguish hold, progress, regress, substitute, deload, rebuild, and cross-domain review needs without relying on a maximal test or chat memory. →S2/W9

- **W6 — Activity and conditioning contour**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: The activity contour may use walking, cycling, VR, conditioning, gym cardio, daily movement, and reduced sedentary time; interest, gameful feedback, and competition may improve adherence but may not override planned effort, recovery, or safety. →S1/S2
  - acceptance: The system can issue a feasible activity option for the current context and can downshift or stop it without treating competition, streaks, or scores as authority. →S2/G-TA-8

- **W7 — Mechanical safety brake**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: New, severe, unusual, persistent, movement-altering, or exertional pain and other concerning symptoms halt progression, prevent intensity escalation, invoke a conservative branch, and use non-diagnostic medical-check language. →S2/S10/S12
  - acceptance: No session brief, guided step, import result, or review may silently continue normal progression while a material safety signal is active. →S2/W4/W11/W12/W9

- **W8 — Bad-week and return path**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Training/activity defines a reduced mode for low time, stress, poor sleep, illness, pain, travel, or motivation loss, plus a non-punitive return path; safety may reduce the prescription to recovery-only or no training. →G-TA-8/S1/S2
  - acceptance: A missed or degraded period yields the next safe feasible action, never forced catch-up, doubled volume, or shame-based escalation. →S2/G-TA-8

- **W9 — Review mutates the system**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Training review consumes normalized LOGs, adherence, biofeedback, pain, completion, technique concerns, boredom, motivation, time friction, tool friction, and owner feedback, then emits one bounded decision: hold, progress, regress, substitute, reduced mode, deload, rebuild, safety escalation, or nutrition-review handoff. →S1/S2/G-TA-3/G-TA-9
  - acceptance: A material pattern changes or deliberately preserves a named authoritative artifact through REVIEW→MUTATION; review is not merely a summary. →S6/S13

### C. Three operational session modes

- **W10 — Equivalent input modes**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: The same planned session may be handled through a session brief plus later report, a specialist-tool result supplied by screenshot/export/text/voice, or a separate guided training chat; all routes converge on one normalized session LOG family. →S11/S15/G-TA-3/G-TA-5
  - acceptance: Review receives equivalent assigned-versus-performed and safety/recovery meaning regardless of the capture route, with unavailable details marked by confidence rather than invented. →G-TA-3/S11

- **W11 — Screenshot and external-result confirmation**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: A screenshot is transient input, not a durable image artifact: the system extracts candidate facts, shows the readable extraction to the owner, accepts correction or confirmation, may ask only materially useful follow-up questions, and then persists the structured normalized result with source and confidence. →S15/S11/G-TA-4
  - acceptance: The screenshot itself is not saved as a durable Health AI or Direction OS artifact; no extracted value becomes trusted durable session data before the owner has had a clear chance to verify or correct it. Missing useful context such as perceived difficulty may be asked conversationally when it changes review value. →S15

- **W12 — Guided training chat**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: A separate guided-session chat loads committed instructions, ACTIVE program/prescription, substitutions, safety rules, and relevant recent state; it presents the next exercise or effort block, accepts natural-language results, records needed signals, and continues until safe completion or stop. →G-TA-5/S10/S11/S13
  - acceptance: The guide may adjust only inside pre-authorized session bounds, may shorten or stop for safety, and may not silently rewrite ACTIVE program authority; a material program change routes to REVIEW→MUTATION. →W7/W9/G-TA-5

- **W13 — Normalized session LOG minimum**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: The normalized record preserves assigned work, performed work, completion, substitutions, effort, relevant recovery and pain signals, source/provenance, confidence, safety disposition, and whether review is needed; exact schema tokens remain a planning decision. →G-TA-3/S1/S2
  - acceptance: A later fresh session can compare prescription to actuals and make the next bounded decision without asking the owner to narrate the session again. →S10/G-TA-3

- **W14 — Guided-session interruption durability**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Owner-confirmed intra-session results must be recoverable enough that a chat interruption does not require re-entering the whole completed portion, while persistence must not become a commit for every repetition; the exact checkpoint boundary is an architecture and planning decision. →G-TA-5/S10/S15
  - acceptance: Converge-arch must demonstrate a file-backed continuation design compatible with the kernel job contract, or return a blocker before shape; provider memory alone cannot satisfy this row. →S6/S10

### D. Tools, cross-domain exchange, and strategic boundary

- **W15 — Specialist-tool authority and fallback**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Hevy-like, Strava-like, VR, and wearable tools may remain authority for their native raw records; Health AI remains authority for the program, safety/review logic, and normalized result. Direct integration is optional, and natural-language or screenshot import remains a valid fallback. →G-TA-1/G-TA-4/S1/S2/S15
  - acceptance: The training process remains usable with no vendor API and no duplication of full native telemetry into Health AI or Direction OS. →S2/S11

- **W16 — Training↔nutrition contract**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Routine dated coupling uses a coarse planned/actual training-demand signal, with actual execution superseding planned execution for that date; sustained volume, phase, deload, strength decline, or recovery concerns may emit a bounded nutrition-review handoff through core-owned review/shared state. Training never edits nutrition artifacts, and nutrition remains sole authority for calorie, protein, menu, recipe, and food-plan changes. →I3/I4/G-TA-9/S1/S2
  - acceptance: Nutrition can respond to training demand without reading raw training telemetry or training files, and training can request nutrition review without writing nutrition state; contract direction, trigger, and minimal payload are frozen in converge-arch. →G-TA-9/S5

- **W17 — Direction OS boundary**
  - status: answered
  - requirement: Raw workout, set, route, pulse, wearable, VR, screenshot, and daily session records are not duplicated into Direction OS; Direction OS stores only strategic summary, problem, decision, incident, evidence pointer, and next CALL while retaining read access when development or review requires it. →I5/S2/S3
  - acceptance: Closing any training job or review does not add raw session data or screenshot media to Direction OS state. →S2
