## 1. Central question

The unresolved fork is not "do we like `ОНО ДЫШИТ`?".

The real question is:

> What should the first core / first proof demonstrate before `q-ono-dyshit-core-frame` returns to canon-forge?

Three branches are alive:

1. **Bottle the Monster / gas capture first** — the first proof is about reading, activating, capturing/sealing and carrying a valuable gas state.
2. **Mission value through gas danger first** — the first proof is about retrieving / rescuing / proving / shutting down / moving mission value through gas as the central danger/tool/route force.
3. **Discriminating hybrid proof** — one shared gas-field spine is compared under two objective skins, so the fork is tested instead of silently chosen.

Out of scope:

- no canon freeze;
- no exact gas roster, gas names, tools, containers, cargo system, timers, economy or progression;
- no creature AI;
- no magical sleep/wake aggro;
- no title-driven mechanics.

`ОНО ДЫШИТ` remains a working title / pitch label only. Title follows canon; canon does not follow title.

## 2. Relevant map slice

Frozen parents and locks that matter:

- `q-gas-role`: gas is a structured field; flagship forms may look alive, but no intent, targeting, pursuit or creature AI.
- `q-gas-value-forms`: visible gas can be collected, but value is state/regime/stability/purity/push/return, not raw volume.
- `q-field-capture-principles`: field capture is bounded state-window capture, not vacuum-volume loot.
- `q-cargo-containment-principles`: captured value is custody / preservation pressure before safe return, not container management.
- `q-coop-shape`: meaningful progression value should create temporary team coverage; no betrayal and no solo all-tools line.
- `q-expedition-macrocycle`: value banks only after safe return; pressure should come from readable return liability, not a universal global timer.
- `q-floor-board-kernel`: floor/cluster must be readable as place + environment + active conditions + actionable read.
- `q-floor-loop`: simple gas work loop is read -> field contact -> gas/value work -> stake/liability changes -> greed/return/deeper decision.
- `q-floor-gas-work-verbs`: extraction/capture is allowed, including large extraction, but anti-vacuum means raw volume cannot be the best line.
- `q-field-legibility`: downstream open requirement; state/value/risk must be read through world signs, sound, instruments and behavior.
- `q-two-player-floor-greybox-proof`: paper cannot certify fun; live two-player proof is the binding gate.

No blocker requires parent reopen now. The problem is routing: if canon-forge resumes before the owner picks a branch, the first proof will choose the branch silently.

## 3. Readiness check

Explore, do not freeze.

The branch is not ready for canon-forge because the owner has not approved which first-proof frame canon-forge should inherit. The correct close is an owner decision point.

## 4. Option A — Bottle the Monster / gas capture first

### Player-action loop

Where we are:

A small floor cluster with a quiet / indirectly readable gas state and a visible route back. The exact gas name and tool are not canon; the proof only needs a valuable state-window in the field.

What players want:

They want to take the gas itself as the prize: read it, make or expose the valuable state, seal/capture a bounded state-window, and return with it before the floor / route / custody situation turns against them.

What players read:

Traces and field signs: layer height, density, front movement, pressure, temperature, residue, sound, instrument needle, route relation, instability, and whether the state is safe-enough / valuable / about to spoil.

What players do:

1. Enter with a rough floor read.
2. Confirm where the gas state is and what keeps it quiet / valuable / dangerous.
3. Physically change the field or building condition: doors, vent, pressure, cold/hot pocket, route isolation, quench, or release.
4. Capture / seal a bounded state-window at the useful moment.
5. Carry it as custody through a return route affected by the same gas work.
6. Decide whether to stabilize, abandon, rescue, or keep greed.

Why risk is tempting:

The gas itself is the prize. The player-facing fantasy is sharp: the air is the monster and the loot.

Why fear exists:

The captured value is still unbanked. It can leak, spoil, lose regime, contaminate shared air, occupy the carrier, worsen the route, or force release before safe return.

What changes:

Gas state changes; a captured state-window exists; a player becomes carrier/custodian; route and shared air may worsen; the team gets a strong greed-vs-return decision.

What can go wrong:

Capture becomes vacuuming; container/cargo stats swallow the proof; exact tools/economy get pulled in; gas becomes a "creature"; wake becomes magic aggro; field-read and physical intervention shrink behind the bottle moment.

### What this option proves

- Whether "gas as prize" is the clearest first fantasy.
- Whether capture/custody/return pressure immediately creates the North-Star feeling: "risked it and pulled it out together".
- Whether a live cargo-like state creates communication, carrier argument, rescue/release choices, and clip-readable stakes.

### What it risks / pulls into scope

- Capture implementation, containers, cargo handling, seal timing, quality/purity, timers, prices/economy and progression.
- It may import pitch material by gravity: named gases, live canister, sleep/wake, seal-at-peak, "gas beast" language.
- It can violate the repair boundary unless every concrete seed stays gated.

### Preserves / violates frozen principles

Preserves if framed as bounded state-window field capture with readable custody and no agency. It aligns with `q-gas-value-forms`, `q-field-capture-principles`, `q-cargo-containment-principles`, `q-coop-shape` and safe-return banking.

Violates if it becomes raw-volume harvesting, exact container/economy design, magic preserved regime tag, creature AI, magical wake, or title-driven "the gas is alive".

Bad, because:

It is the strongest hook, but the most dangerous first canon branch: it can make the whole game a container/cargo game before gas as field, floor read, and mission-value grammar are independently proven.

## 5. Option B — Mission value through gas danger first

### Player-action loop

Where we are:

A floor cluster with a non-gas mission value: recover object/data/proof, rescue/return a body/person/sample, reach a shutdown/control point, mark a procedure, or open a route. The value can only be reached, preserved, or returned by understanding gas.

What players want:

They want to complete a plain mission while gas is the main system: threat, clue, tool, route shaper, accident cause, co-op pressure and optional value source.

What players read:

Hidden/quiet atmosphere signs, active conditions, source/flow, route danger, object/person/proof location, and how gas/building/condition changes will affect return.

What players do:

1. Enter with mission objective plus rough gas read.
2. Build a working hypothesis through movement, observation and bounded tests.
3. Intervene physically: isolate, vent, redirect, quench, open/close, break/route, use/avoid condition.
4. Make the mission value reachable or preserve it through gas consequence.
5. Carry / escort / record / trigger / return the mission value.
6. Choose one-more-room / deeper / return based on route and liability.

Why risk is tempting:

The valuable thing is behind or inside a gas situation. One more room, one more signal, one more route change or one more proof can pay.

Why fear exists:

Gas can worsen the route, hurt players, spoil the mission value, cut off return, force rescue, or turn the team around.

What changes:

The floor state, route, player safety, mission value and return liability change because of gas work. The team wins by making gas legible and actionable, not by merely avoiding fog.

What can go wrong:

Gas becomes background hazard; objective becomes ordinary salvage; market hook weakens; capture fantasy gets deferred so far it loses energy; proof may feel like "retrieve item through danger" unless gas-specific decisions are unmistakable.

### What this option proves

- Whether gas is gameplay before any cargo/container implementation.
- Whether the Simple Gas Work Loop can carry a mission: read -> intervene -> consequence -> return liability.
- Whether non-gas value can keep gas central through field shaping and route/player/value stakes.

### What it risks / pulls into scope

- Mission vocabulary and contract grammar.
- Strong field-legibility and route consequence requirements.
- It may need a sharper hook line so the proof does not become generic hazmat salvage.

### Preserves / violates frozen principles

Preserves the current floor-loop, macrocycle, anti-vacuum, no-AI and no-economy boundaries best. It keeps capture/cargo downstream while still letting gas remain threat/tool/value source.

Violates if gas is only damage aura or decor; if value is reachable without gas-specific read/intervention; or if it turns into ordinary salvage with gas wallpaper.

Bad, because:

It is the safest canon branch, but the weakest pitch branch. It can protect the system and still fail the player-facing "why should friends care?" test.

## 6. Option C — Discriminating hybrid proof

### Player-action loop

Where we are:

One tiny shared gas-field spine: same floor cluster, same source/flow/condition problem, same route back, same two-player pressure. Only the objective skin changes.

Pass 1:

Mission value through gas danger. The team retrieves / rescues / proves / shuts down something non-gas through the gas problem.

Pass 2:

Gas capture value. The team uses the same gas problem to capture / preserve / return a bounded gas state.

What players want:

They want to discover which objective makes the same gas-field work feel more like the real game.

What players read:

The same field signs in both passes: gas location, layer, movement, source, active condition, route risk, instability, and return liability.

What players do:

1. Compare the same readable gas situation under two goals.
2. Track which goal makes better decisions, communication, greed/fear, co-op role pressure and replay pull.
3. Stop before exact roster/tools/containers/economy.

Why risk is tempting:

The hybrid exposes the real discriminant: is the exciting part "bring value through gas" or "the gas itself is the value"?

Why fear exists:

Both passes use the same field and return-risk substrate; failure remains attributable and gas-driven.

What changes:

The project gets a branch decision without canon freeze. The proof tells canon-forge what it may inherit.

What can go wrong:

Hybrid can sprawl, become two prototypes, or pull in exact capture/cargo tools anyway. If built too large, it solves everything badly.

### What this option proves

- Which branch better creates visible player action, communication and stake.
- Whether Bottle-first's market hook is worth its scope pull.
- Whether mission-value-first can keep gas central enough.
- Whether a later grey-box should start with capture, mission value, or a paired A/B proof.

### What it risks / pulls into scope

- It needs a strict cut: one cluster, one gas-field problem, one mission-value skin, one gas-capture skin.
- It may require one placeholder capture/custody affordance, but not a container system.
- It must not certify fun on paper.

### Preserves / violates frozen principles

Preserves the repair boundary best because it refuses to silently choose. It keeps title/pitch seeds gated and uses frozen principles as comparison criteria.

Violates if "hybrid" becomes a full implementation plan, exact tool/container/economy design, or a way to delay the owner decision forever.

Bad, because:

It is wider than either branch. Its value depends entirely on discipline: if the cut is not tiny, it becomes a scope spill instead of a fork test.

## 7. Stress through mechanic lenses

### Grows from core

Bottle-first grows from field capture + custody + safe return, but can become bottle fantasy pasted over the field if capture is not a consequence of gas work.

Mission-value-first grows from floor loop and expedition macrocycle, but fails if gas can be replaced by any hazard.

Hybrid grows from the unresolved fork itself and compares both under the same gas-field spine.

### Interesting decision

Bottle-first has the clearest greed object: seal/carry the valuable state or release/stabilize/leave. It risks dominant "always capture" if mission alternatives vanish.

Mission-value-first can create strong decisions only if gas changes route/value/player state, not just HP.

Hybrid tests which branch creates the stronger no-dominance choice.

### Anti-solo / co-op

Bottle-first has natural carrier/reader/route-holder roles, but may become one skilled player + cargo busywork.

Mission-value-first needs explicit live shared-state coupling: one player's route/gas action must change the other's situation.

Hybrid should use the same two-player pressure in both passes.

### Pillar pull

Bottle-first pulls hard toward "we pulled it out together".

Mission-value-first pulls toward "we survived / solved / rescued through gas"; it needs greed/value to avoid pure emergency response.

Hybrid measures the pillar pull instead of assuming it.

### Field-read

All options fail if players cannot read gas state before irreversible loss. `q-field-legibility` remains downstream required work.

### Grey-box

All options remain PAPER / UNVERIFIED. Only later two-player grey-box can certify fun.

## 8. Recommendation

Recommendation: **choose the constrained hybrid as the next route before canon-forge resumes**.

Reason:

The fork is real and branch choice will shape the first playable proof. Bottle-first is the strongest market/clip fantasy, but it pulls capture/cargo/economy too early. Mission-value-first is the cleanest canon-safe frame, but it may undercut the hook and become ordinary salvage. A constrained hybrid is the only option that preserves the owner's correction: compare instead of silently freezing.

Strict recommendation shape:

- Not a big prototype.
- Not a canon card.
- Not exact tools/containers/economy.
- One shared gas-field scene.
- Two objective skins.
- Owner decides after seeing the comparison whether canon-forge inherits Bottle-first, mission-value-first, or a two-layer frame.

If owner refuses hybrid and wants an immediate canon branch, the safer fallback is **mission value through gas danger first**, with Bottle the Monster preserved as a high-value downstream contract / first hook candidate. Bottle-first should become the immediate branch only if the owner explicitly accepts early capture/custody scope and the risk of cargo pulling the design.

## 9. Explicit owner decision needed

Before canon-forge resumes, owner must choose:

1. **Bottle-first now.** Accept early capture/custody pull; canon-forge frames first proof around gas as prize.
2. **Mission-value-first now.** Keep first canon frame on gas danger/tool/route/value through a non-gas mission objective; preserve capture as later/high-value branch.
3. **Constrained hybrid first (recommended).** Run/forge the smallest discriminating comparison before canon-forge freezes inheritance.

No branch is frozen by this note.

- creature AI;

- harvest-first law;

- automatic import of pitch/research examples.

If a mechanic needs the title to make sense, it fails.
