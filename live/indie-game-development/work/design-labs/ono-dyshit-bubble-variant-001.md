## 4. Bubble player-action loop

Where we are:

A small floor cluster with sleeping / quiet / indirectly readable gas pools or pockets. The exact gas names from the pitch remain candidate examples only.

What players want:

Players want to find a valuable sleeping gas pocket, wrap a membrane bubble around a bounded state, and carry that visible gas-body back to safe return without rupture.

What players read:

Before capture:

- traces of sleeping gas: residue, frost, dust pull, layer height, instrument signal, pressure/temperature signs;
- route shape: doors, stairs, vents, hot pipes, sharp geometry, draft, narrow passages;
- whether the pocket is cheap/safe/near or deep/valuable/risky.

During custody:

- bubble size, tension, wobble, opacity, pulse, pressure, drift, buoyancy, deformation, contact with heat/sharp edges, and how the contained gas behaves.

What players do:

1. Scout sleeping gas and choose which pocket is worth taking.
2. Plan a return route before taking it.
3. Place a membrane ring / capture affordance around a bounded gas state.
4. Bubble inflates and becomes visible live custody.
5. Players carry, steer, rotate, duck, hold doors, avoid heat/sharp edges/drafts, and coordinate route movement.
6. If it ruptures, gas releases into the room and re-enters the floor simulation; players may retreat, rescue, re-bubble if possible, or abandon.
7. Greed loop: after one bubble is safe, decide whether to go back for another while the floor is more awake / worse.

Why risk is tempting:

The value is immediately legible: the team is literally carrying the gas prize. Bigger/deeper/rarer/more active bubbles promise more value and better stories.

Why fear exists:

The prize is fragile, visible and local. A mistake does not become a menu penalty; it releases gas here, next to the team, into the same route they need.

What changes:

- a bounded gas state becomes live custody;
- players' bodies/attention/route are occupied;
- the floor can wake or worsen after capture;
- rupture changes the world and can trigger normal gas spread/reactions;
- return liability becomes visible as a fragile object moving through geometry.

What can go wrong:

- bubble becomes a generic physics-cargo gimmick, "R.E.P.O. with balloons";
- membrane ring becomes a hardcoded magic tool unrelated to gas laws;
- exact soft-body physics and collision implementation swallow design;
- every gas becomes "same fragile sphere" unless type behavior changes bubble handling;
- bubble carry becomes mandatory full-team busywork for every cheap sample;
- fun is assumed from paper.

What remains unresolved:

- exact capture affordance;
- bubble size limits;
- how gas properties map to buoyancy/drag/tension/rupture;
- whether bubble is held by hands, tether, frame, ring, or route rig;
- how much soft-body simulation is required;
- what signals show pressure/instability;
- how re-bubbling after rupture works;
- exact economy/value model.

## 5. Why this is stronger than generic Bottle-first

Generic canister/bottle capture risks hiding the gas behind metal and moving the game into container stats. `Пузырь` does the opposite:

- the cargo remains visible gas;
- the contained gas still expresses type/condition through movement;
- failure returns the gas to the world instead of deleting value;
- custody is readable without UI;
- co-op is body/route/voice based, not inventory based;
- the childhood "carry a balloon without popping it" model is instantly understood.

So `Пузырь` should be treated as the strongest current **Bottle/Bubble capture-first form**, not as a separate world ontology.

## 6. Stress through mechanic lenses

### Grows from core

Paper pass, with a warning.

It grows from shared gas field + extraction + custody: players bound a visible gas state and carry it through the same world. If the membrane is only a fantasy skin over arbitrary balloon physics, it fails. It must derive from gas properties: density, pressure, temperature, flow, instability and state-window preservation.

### Interesting decision

Strong.

The visible decision is clean:

- take a small near bubble now or risk a deeper valuable one;
- preserve current bubble or go back for another;
- choose safe long route or dangerous short route;
- abandon / release / re-bubble / rescue after rupture.

Bad form: largest possible bubble always best. The design needs non-monotone risk: bigger/deeper/rarer is harder to route and preserve.

### Anti-solo

Promising but not certified.

Large bubble naturally creates temporary roles: front/back carriers, scout, door/vent operator, heat/sharp-edge guard, rescue/re-bubble. It still needs anti-solo control: small cheap bubbles may be solo, but meaningful progression bubbles should not be sequentially soloable by one skilled player.

### Pillar pull

Very strong.

"We carried it out together" is literal. It preserves greed/fear because the prize remains dangerous in the team's hands.

### Field-read

Strong if visual language works.

The bubble can show state directly: pulsing, drift, sagging, lift, tension, clouding, glow, shiver, pressure. It fails if players need hidden numbers to know when it will rupture.

### Grey-box

UNVERIFIED.

This needs the later two-player test. Paper cannot prove that carrying the bubble is fun rather than fiddly cargo handling.

Recommendation: **Bubble-informed hybrid first**.

Why:

The Bubble idea is strong enough that the old generic Bottle-first comparison is now obsolete. But choosing Bubble-first immediately would still freeze the first proof around a concrete tool/transfer substrate. The safest high-signal route is:

- keep mission-value-first alive;
- replace generic Bottle-first with Bubble-capture-first;
- run/forge a tiny discriminating comparison where Bubble is the capture-side objective.

If owner wants the bolder branch:

Choose **Bubble-first now**, but explicitly accept that first proof will pull membrane/custody/route-carry behavior into scope and must not solve economy, exact gas roster, final container system or full soft-body tech.
