## Selected answer — A: Control Obligation Grammar

The current minimum common gas-behavior layer is movement/control.

Every gameplay gas must have at least one primary readable physical
movement tendency that creates a Control Obligation inside the shared
Sphere extraction/custody system.

A Control Obligation is the physical work the player must perform,
anticipate or exploit because of the gas's movement behavior.

The current minimal job grammar is:

1. Counter

   Oppose or exploit a directed physical tendency.

   This job changes where players position themselves, from which side
   they apply force, which clearance they choose and whether the
   tendency should be resisted or used.

2. Brake

   Manage retained movement, acceleration, stopping and redirection.

   This job changes required braking space, approach speed, route
   choice and when players must begin stopping or changing direction.

3. Time

   Act relative to a visibly recurring physical impulse or weakening
   of force.

   This job changes when players close, pull, push or pass a physical
   constraint. It does not require a timing meter or hidden random
   cycle.

These are gameplay-job families, not gas names and not a final roster.
The answer does not determine the exact number of gases.

## Gameplay-job admission test

A proposed movement behavior passes this paper answer only when all are
true:

1. It can be explained as one readable physical rule.
2. It works through the same standard Sphere extraction/custody
   system.
3. Replacing the behavior in the same physical situation changes at
   least one meaningful decision about:
   - extraction position;
   - affected part of the field;
   - chosen volume;
   - stopping point;
   - body/strap positioning;
   - timing;
   - braking or redirection;
   - return route.
4. The difference is not merely:
   - another name or colour;
   - a different numerical strength;
   - the same directed job rotated to another compass direction;
   - a gas-specific shell requirement;
   - gas-type damage to the shell;
   - a reaction, damage outcome or special tool standing in for the
     movement job.
5. The consequence remains attributable to the gas's physical
   tendency, geometry and player action.

Volume remains the strength axis. A stronger expression of the same
Control Obligation is not automatically a new job family.

Shell remains the general-endurance axis. No Control Obligation creates
gas-specific shell eligibility or a gas × shell matrix.

## One-player handling and help

This grammar does not impose a mandatory N-player gate.

- One player can control a small Sphere.
- One player may attempt a somewhat larger Sphere when route, speed,
  positioning and available control margin permit it.
- The price of doing more alone may be slower handling, earlier
  stopping, a longer route, less correction margin or inability to
  exploit an advanced manoeuvre.
- Additional players add bounded force from other directions, earlier
  braking positions and live timing coordination.
- Additional players improve controllability; they do not create a
  binary gas/Sphere eligibility rule.

Under the three jobs:

- Counter can be handled alone at small force by choosing a favourable
  side and route.
- Brake can be handled alone by reducing speed and allowing more
  stopping distance.
- Time can be handled alone by waiting for or using a favourable
  physical phase.

Exact one-player limits, helper count, scaling and carry tuning remain
OPEN.

## Movement-first, not movement-only

This answer deliberately resolves only the minimum movement/control
grammar.

It does not state that all future meaningful gas differences must be
movement effects.

Future gases or contained Spheres may have additional effects or
modifiers, provided that later bounded design work selects them.

Those future effects:

- do not receive authority from this artifact;
- do not substitute for the required movement job;
- do not silently create gas × shell compatibility;
- do not define damage, reactions, status effects or implementation
  here.

The owner's reference to something like a radioactive Sphere is an
example of a possible future non-movement effect dimension only. This
artifact selects no radiation rule, consequence, damage law or
modifier taxonomy.

## Parent locks preserved

- The Sphere is the active capture object and visible container.
- Extraction begins near gameplay gas.
- Existing gas moves visibly into a growing Sphere.
- The player chooses where to begin, how much to take and when to
  close.
- The closed Sphere remains free physical cargo.
- Short straps provide sustained control.
- Bodies provide short physical impulses.
- Shell = general physical endurance.
- Gas = physical direction/tendency.
- Volume = strength.
- Every standard Sphere captures and holds every gameplay gas.
- Gas type alone cannot damage, weaken, destabilize or rupture it.
- No mandatory gas-specific Sphere category or gas × shell matrix
  exists.
- Mixed-gas consequences remain undefined common gas × gas rules.
- Accepted Frames remain non-canon.
