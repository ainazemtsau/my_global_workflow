# Market research — how a pre-audience solo indie dev bootstraps first wishlists

Produced 2026-06-16 for node **g-2f8c** (the marketing track), during map c-map-003.
Method: 9-agent deep-research workflow (wf_c85f223d-c09) — 5 web-research angles → adversarial verification
of decision-critical claims → synthesis. Sources are real cases (Chris Zukowski / howtomarketagame,
GameDiscoverCo, Steam Next Fest data, named dev postmortems). **Read this before shaping g-2f8c — do NOT
re-derive the playbook.**

Subject: a SOLO dev, NO existing audience, low appetite for manual social media, building a systemic CO-OP
game (real-time multi-gas grid sim, destructible topology, expedition/horror; SS13/Barotrauma/Stationeers
lineage; low-poly stylized; gallows-deadpan tone). Fixed calendar: Steam page ~2026-08-31, Next Fest ~Oct
2026, first-money signal 2026-12-11. Cash ~$3k total.

## Bottom line

Go **GAMEPLAY-FIRST**, read precisely:
- Spend the highest-ROI **~$300 on a professional ILLUSTRATED CAPSULE**, and lead the trailer / screenshots /
  in-page GIFs with **REAL emergent systemic moments**. Concept/vision-only content warms a cold start but
  converts weak, low-intent wishlists (REANIMAL: 2.7% week-1 off 408k showcase wishlists, vs hands-on demos
  ~37.5% demo→wishlist).
- Channel/positioning work CAN profitably start before "finished gameplay" exists, but **only the cheap,
  no-decay CAPTURE layer is pure upside NOW**: a live Steam Coming-Soon page (the moment the look is locked +
  one real-gameplay GIF), the illustrated capsule, a "beta-key-for-email" mailing list, one reserved studio
  handle. Do NOT post filler before a striking sim clip (the only thing the "dead-account" caution is right
  about). Skip charisma-dependent YouTube devlogs.
- The binding constraint is the **BUILD**: every channel + the whole calendar is gated on getting ONE
  legible, GIF-able systemic moment (gas/fire/flood/sabotage cascade) on screen. Make THAT the first
  publishable material, dropped onto a live, illustrated-capsule Steam page the same week.

## Concept-first vs gameplay-first

GAMEPLAY-FIRST for conversion-grade material. For a systemic SS13/Barotrauma-lineage game the differentiator
(real-time multi-gas grid, destructible topology) is **inherently footage-dependent** — depth does not read
in static art; the appeal IS the simulation visibly cascading. The illustrated CAPSULE is the one place
concept-art quality directly wins wishlists. (Uncertainty: no clean controlled "footage vs concept art" A/B
exists; "substantially better" = expert consensus + case data.)

## Can channel work start before gameplay?

YES — but only the cheap CAPTURE layer is pure upside; an active content grind before you have a striking
visual clip is low-yield (opportunity cost), NOT a "dead-account curse." The dead-account penalty targets
accounts that build a following then go silent, or buy ghost followers — NOT a fresh handle posting
infrequently. The binding variable is whether you have a **striking visual clip**, not a "finished game."
A gas-sim/destructible-topology game produces GIF-able moments well before it's finished. (2025-2026 caveat:
TikTok organic devlog reach collapsed in early 2025 → strengthens "passive capture + mailing list first.")

## Minimal pure-upside setup (do these now / soon)

1. Stand up the Steam **Coming-Soon page** the moment the art style is locked + ONE strong real-gameplay GIF.
   Pages up 6+ months pre-launch earn ~300% more sales; wishlists do NOT age. No penalty/boost for an early
   empty page.
2. Commission a professional **illustrated capsule (~$300)** — the single highest-ROI dollar; the game in
   ~90% of impressions.
3. **Mailing list** with "beta key in exchange for your email" — highest-leverage cheap early asset; it holds
   virality you cannot otherwise keep. (Failbetter: one email → 1,200 wishlists.)
4. Reserve ONE persistent **studio handle** (not per-game) — a following is a cross-game snowball.
5. Begin posting ONLY when you have a genuinely striking sim clip; treat all social as a funnel INTO the
   mailing list. No filler.

## Channels ranked (this genre)

1. **The Steam page itself** — the actual conversion engine (capsule + tags + screenshots = 68-88% of all
   wishlist conversion). Must be excellent before driving any traffic.
2. **Reddit** — best cold-start spike for legible visuals (one 30s gameplay GIF drove Laysara 11k wishlists
   in <24h). Niche subs for steady traffic; obey the 10% self-promo rule. Gated on a legible gameplay GIF.
3. **YouTube/Twitch streamers + short-form spillover** — HIGHEST ceiling for co-op emergent-chaos games
   (Content Warning 6.2M downloads/24h; Lethal Company ~9.4M, near-zero press). Gated on a stable multiplayer
   demo (later lever). Balatro playbook: direct-outreach to ~hundreds of SMALL streamers who play adjacent
   games (Slay the Spire / SS13 / Barotrauma).
4. **TikTok/Shorts/Reels** — free velocity CATALYST (spikes wishlist velocity → Popular Upcoming → Discovery
   Queue), not a direct wishlist source. Poor fit for low social-media appetite unless batched.
5. **Steam Next Fest (~Oct 2026)** — a MULTIPLIER of momentum you already built, NOT a discovery engine.
   Entry wishlist count is the dominant predictor (r=0.825). Launch the demo WEEKS early (~2.5x more wishlists).
6. **Discord + mailing list + X** — bottom tier for ACQUISITION; they convert already-interested people into
   day-1 buyers (retention/spike). (Mailing list is top-tier as a CAPTURE asset, just not acquisition.)

## Realistic runway

Tight. Page-up ~08-31 → Next Fest ~Oct = only ~4-6 weeks pre-fest. Organic wishlist gain from zero is slow
(even hyped games cap ~300/day; a healthy page clears >150 in its first 2 weeks). ~7-10k pre-fest is the right
but ambitious target from a cold start; plan realistically to ENTER Next Fest with low-single-thousands +
use an early-released, high-converting demo (target 20%+ play-and-wishlist; median 16.3%) as the multiplier.
**De-risking upside:** co-op/"crewlike" games convert wishlists→sales at 68-266x the ~0.15-0.20x median
(PEAK 266x, Mage Arena 78x, R.E.P.O. 68x) via friend-group word-of-mouth — players buy in clusters. This
lineage sits squarely in that bucket, so a decent Dec-11 money signal does NOT require a giant wishlist pile.
Dominant launch swing factors: REVIEW SCORE (winners ~91% Very Positive in 7 days vs 67%) and a SHORTER
pre-release time. A genuinely good, demonstrable game beats a long marketing runway.

## First publishable material (recommendation)

A single tight package centered on ONE instantly-legible, GIF-able systemic moment — NOT concept-art, NOT
lore, NOT a charisma YouTube devlog: (1) a 20-30s real-gameplay GIF of one system visibly cascading
(emergent gas/fire/flood/sabotage chain in the co-op grid), readable in the first 5s; (2) published the same
week onto a LIVE Steam Coming-Soon page fronted by the ~$300 illustrated capsule (telegraphs genre/tone at a
glance); (3) with a "beta-key-for-email" mailing-list/Discord capture wired in, seeded to one well-aligned
niche subreddit. Launch before polish — wishlists don't age and a viral moment with no live page is wasted.
Spend the $3k on the capsule + a tight demo, NOT on ads.

## Key risks

1. **Build-gating (the binding constraint):** everything is gated on ONE legible GIF-able systemic moment. If
   the build can't produce a glanceable emergent clip by mid-2026, the marketing plan has nothing to run on.
   The genre's "bowling-ball" risk (depth doesn't read in a glance) → deliberately ENGINEER one shareable
   single-glance moment per system, don't over-explain.
2. **Theater / vanity metrics:** concept art / lore posts / YouTube devlogs feel productive but front-load
   low-intent wishlists + risk the "developer-audience trap" (attracting non-buying devs).
3. **Dead-account framing is OVERSTATED** but the caution holds for FILLER: a quiet fresh account is fine; low-
   value filler posting is the real waste.
4. **Runway:** ~4-6 weeks page→fest from a cold start is tight for 7-10k; Next Fest is a multiplier not a
   discovery engine; drop the demo WEEKS early (surprise-dropping mid-fest = ~2.5x penalty).
5. **Evidence quality:** "real footage out-converts concept art" is consensus + case data, not a controlled
   A/B; "Next Fest = biggest pre-launch driver" is refuted as worded (LAUNCH DAY is the biggest single event;
   Next Fest amplifies momentum).
6. **Capsule under-investment:** skimping on the ~$300 illustrated capsule is the worst false economy.
