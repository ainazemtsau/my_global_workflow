## 1. Player promise

Players repeatedly enter a space containing nearly or completely invisible gas,
investigate enough to form a usable plan, conduct a risky extraction/mining/
operation and then evacuate people and value through an environment their own
actions may already have changed. [F1, A1, A2, A3, E1]

The tension is not "watch the simulation run." Players commit to a plan under
incomplete knowledge; their actions change gas, space and the return situation;
the resulting consequences test the plan and force adaptation. [F1, P1, A3]

Current verdict: KEEP under owner verdict "FRAME READY".

## 2. Global loop

`staging/preparation → descent → investigation → plan → extraction/mining/
operation → evacuation/return, retreat or failure → banked result, knowledge or
readiness → another descent` [A1, E1]

The crew enters to obtain gas-linked value or readiness, but this frame does not
decide exact money, recipes, upgrades or progression. [E1, O1]

Going deeper is rational when current understanding, preparation and return
capacity support it. Returning or retreating is rational when the operation,
activated gas or changed route makes the unbanked stake too dangerous. [A3, E1]

What may carry forward is the safely returned result plus knowledge/readiness.
Exact persistence, insurance, loss and economy remain open. [E1, O1]

Current verdict: KEEP broad loop; DEFER exact meta and progression.

## 3. Local repeatable loop

`understand enough → form a plan → act → gas/space/team state changes →
adapt the plan` [A1, A2, A3, F1]

Investigation, operation and evacuation are not required to be three clean,
scripted acts. Careless action may activate the gas during investigation; the
crew then executes, revises or abandons an incomplete plan inside the same
continuing situation. [A3]

At frame altitude, repeated decisions are:

- continue investigating or commit to the operation;
- continue extracting or protect the evacuation route;
- adapt, abandon value, retreat or attempt the return.

Exact information, instruments, controls, thresholds and operation verbs remain
separate questions. [O1]

Current verdict: KEEP the loop; DEFER its detailed mechanics.

## 4. Core interaction roles

- player ↔ gas:
  investigate enough to plan, then interact with gas-linked value during the
  operation and evacuation; exact detection, extraction, release and carry verbs
  remain OPEN. [A1, A2, O1]

- player ↔ space/grid:
  read and choose an operation/return route, use or alter spatial relations and
  adapt when topology or access changes. [F1, A1]

- gas ↔ space/grid:
  space and topology determine where gas can accumulate, move and affect the
  route. [F1]

- gas ↔ gas/reactions:
  reactions may alter the operation, danger, value and evacuation situation.
  Exact roster and reaction rules remain OPEN. [F1, O1]

- destruction/changeable space ↔ gas:
  changing space changes gas flow and route structure; it may help the plan or
  create the catastrophe the crew must survive. [F1, A3]

These are five roles inside one game loop, not five separately required
minigames.

Current verdict: KEEP at frame altitude; DEFER exact mechanisms.

## 5. Why co-op

The frame contains a credible source of live shared pressure:

- investigation requires understanding a distributed, initially unreadable
  situation;
- operation consumes attention, position or hands;
- evacuation requires preserving or recovering a route while the same shared
  field changes;
- one player's gas or space action can change another player's immediate
  situation. [A1, A2, A3, C1]

This is not yet a proved anti-solo mechanic. Exact roles, tool restrictions,
simultaneous dependencies and bypass resistance remain OPEN. Multiple players
alone do not satisfy the co-op requirement. [C1, O1]

Current verdict: KEEP as a credible frame-level source plus an explicit OPEN gap.
Actual two-player interdependence remains UNVERIFIED.

## 6. Escalation, failure, retreat and replay

Situations may worsen because players activate the gas too early, execute an
incomplete plan, trigger movement or reactions, alter topology, lose a route or
create a dangerous extraction/evacuation state. [A3, F1]

A mistake should normally create a continuing state of play: activated gas,
changed flow, released or lost value, a damaged return route, rescue pressure,
forced adaptation or retreat. It need not be only a punishment screen. [P1, E1]

Safe return converts the attempt into retained result/readiness. Retreat with
little or no value may be correct. Another attempt is attractive because players
retain understanding/readiness while gas, space, activation and route situations
can differ. [E1]

Exact death, damage, recovery, reset, procedural variation and rewards remain
OPEN. [O1]

Current verdict: KEEP broad behavior; DEFER exact consequences and replay model.

## 7. Explicit non-scope

This frame does not decide:

- exact meaning of "find" or "detect" gas;
- instruments, signals, evidence types or information UI;
- exact sleeping/active gas states and transitions;
- exact triggers or timing of premature activation;
- the threshold for sufficient investigation;
- exact extraction/mining/operation mechanics;
- whether Bubble is the default, optional or special extraction method;
- Bubble filling, physics, capacity, carry controls or rupture rules;
- exact mission objectives or gas-work operation catalogue;
- gas roster, reaction catalogue or recipes;
- damage, health, downed, rescue, death, recovery or reset;
- tools, controls, inventory, UI or onboarding;
- economy, prices, progression, insurance or campaign length;
- numbers, tuning, balance or difficulty scaling;
- implementation, networking, VFX, content production or engine architecture;
- empirical fun, actual anti-solo play, runtime legibility, engine cost or
  commercial conversion. [O1]

Current verdict: DEFER all listed questions.
