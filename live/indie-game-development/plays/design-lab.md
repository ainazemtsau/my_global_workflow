# Play: local/design-lab

Purpose: explore one complex design/canon question before `local/canon-forge`, when the answer is not ready to freeze because it depends on gameplay feel, adjacent questions, blockers, parent-canon risk, or optional visual proof. Design Lab produces a routed next step, not frozen canon.

Reads: the chosen question/map note, frozen parents, relevant open adjacent questions, `knowledge/mechanic-lenses.md`, and visual/style files only if visual checking is needed.

Writes: a plain work note under `work/design-labs/`, child/open question proposals, map updates, LOG line, and a next CALL. It does not freeze canon and does not edit NOW/TREE/CHARTER.

## Rules

- No hardcoded counts. The session considers exactly the adjacent questions, scenes, options, or examples needed for understanding; no quota.
- Owner-facing text comes first in plain language: what we are trying to understand, why it matters, what is known, what is not being solved.
- Terms must be translated into gameplay. A term without “player wants / sees / does / risks / changes” is not a design result.
- If the work exposes a blocker, stop and route to the blocker. If it exposes a parent-canon problem, route to parent reopen/repair. Do not patch around it silently.
- Visual check is optional: use it only when a diagram/image is needed to verify understanding or readability. Visuals do not certify fun.
- Paper results are UNVERIFIED. Fun requires later grey-box evidence when relevant.

## Steps

1. **Frame** — state the central question in owner language; name what success would clarify and what is out of scope.
2. **Map slice** — show only the relevant map slice: parents, blockers, adjacent questions that truly affect this question, and possible parent-reopen risks.
3. **Readiness check** — decide whether to explore, stop for blocker, repair the map, or reopen a parent.
4. **Explore** — produce understandable gameplay scenes/models. Each useful scene must say: where we are, what players want, what they read, what they do, why risk is tempting, why fear exists, what changes, what can go wrong, and what remains unresolved.
5. **Stress** — check against mechanic lenses at paper altitude: grows from core, interesting decision, anti-solo risk if relevant, pillar pull, field-read. Do not claim fun.
6. **Route** — choose the next route: canon-forge ready, continue lab, blocker CALL, map repair CALL, parent reopen CALL, visual check CALL, grey-box/prototype CALL, or capture only.
7. **Close** — emit RESULT with readable owner summary first, then state changes and next CALL.

## Done when

One work note exists with the lab result; LOG line is proposed; next route is explicit and self-contained. If canon-forge is next, it carries the owner-understood direction and boundaries. If not, the blocker/repair/reopen reason is explicit.
