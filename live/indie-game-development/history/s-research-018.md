concept_architecture_summary: |
  ONE-PAGE CONCEPT SPINE

  Working spine:
  A co-op team repeatedly enters a huge abandoned underground base-instrument through a vertical shaft/lift spine, descends
  from a cold mostly-safe upper zone into a warmer active working depth, reads a structured multi-regime gas field, creates
  or finds valuable controlled outcomes, and must bring the result back out together. The pressure is always:
  "we can leave and bank this" vs "we can push deeper for a better tier / stronger clue / more dangerous regime".

  Frozen anchors:
  - Player promise: "Мы рискнули — и вытащили это вместе".
  - Gas is the star: danger + loot + spectacle come from simulation, not scripts.
  - Co-op must be structural: no solo-with-friends, no parallel-solo scavenging.
  - Depth is not a damage slider: depth changes environmental conditions and gas regime.
  - The base is vertical: cold upper zone → active dangerous depth → cold near-inert terminus.
  - The end exists: there is a reachable bottom / recorded answer, but exact q-the-deep content is not needed yet.

  Non-frozen but useful hypothesis:
  - The shaft/lift is the strongest current physical spine.
  - One expedition may span multiple loaded floors.
  - A surface return may be the save/bank boundary.
  - State may carry between floors: loot, tools, damage, contamination, route condition, or other risk.
  These are raw material only until q-expedition-macrocycle is forged.

  Anti-spine / do not inherit:
  - Do not turn the game into raw gas mining / bottle-selling.
  - Do not use arbitrary locks, quotas, keyhunts, or "collect X to open deeper".
  - Do not freeze biome taxonomy before room jobs and loop jobs exist.
  - Do not make gas a monster with will, targeting, or chase intent.
  - Do not treat gas fronts, stabilization, route verbs, valves, breach/vent details from s-canon-015 as accepted mechanics.
  - Do not let "the sim runs" count as fun; later mechanics must pass mechanic lenses and eventually a two-player grey-box.

  0. Frozen parent substrate — already exists
     Core question:
       What is the game promise and physical world that lower loops must inherit?
     Established answer:
       North Star + gas role + city: co-op descent, shared gas field, vertical base, environmental gate, reachable bottom.
     Why it precedes lower levels:
       Lower loops must grow from frozen constraints instead of inventing mechanics and backfilling lore.
     Decides:
       Promise, tone, gas ontology, city verticality, no-solo anti-goal, no gas teleology.
     Must not decide:
       Exact expedition structure, floor gameplay, economy, UI, room verbs, balance.

  1. q-full-game-descent-arc — ADD / FIRST NEXT
     Core question:
       What is one complete game arc from first entry to "we reached the end / bottom", and why does the team want to go
       all the way down rather than only farm runs?
     Why it must precede lower levels:
       "Go deeper or leave" has no meaning until "deeper toward what" and "finish means what" exist.
     Decides:
       Full-game phases, endpoint role, how loot/depth/story relate, whether the game has a final terminus path, what counts
       as strategic progress toward the bottom.
     Must not decide:
       Exact q-the-deep lore content, floor verbs, gas families, equipment stats, prices, UI, exact mission count.

  2. q-between-expeditions-progression — ADD
     Core question:
       After a completed or failed expedition, what changes permanently or semi-permanently?
     Why it must precede lower levels:
       Extraction stakes, failure stakes, and "banking" only matter if the project knows what can be saved, lost, unlocked,
       contaminated, repaired, or learned between runs.
     Decides:
       Persistence categories: banked value, map knowledge, access/depth readiness, equipment/preparation, crew/base state,
       failure consequences, what "return to surface" commits.
     Must not decide:
       Economy numbers, upgrade tree details, exact items, UI screens, vendor/shop implementation.

  3. q-depth-grammar — ADD
     Core question:
       Why is deeper qualitatively different, necessary, and harder in gameplay terms?
     Why it must precede lower levels:
       An expedition cannot make a clean push/leave decision if "lower" is only "same rooms with bigger damage".
     Decides:
       Depth rules as gameplay grammar: regime flips, preparation mismatch, tier/value escalation, route-back cost,
       erased floors/frontier, environmental gates, what kinds of readiness deeper demands.
     Must not decide:
       Exact gas-family roster, exact reaction table, exact floor hazards, encounter pacing, final balance.

  3b. q-coop-shape — RESUME / REFRAME AS CROSS-CUTTING PARENT
     Core question:
       What hard co-op pressure makes the descent structurally not solo-with-friends?
     Why it must precede expedition/floor/mechanics:
       Every expedition/floor/mechanic must know what kind of interdependence it is obligated to create.
     Decides:
       Shared extraction/carry boundary, non-local operation, rescue/failure coupling, route/return dependency, or a hybrid.
     Must not decide:
       Full-game endpoint, exact floor layout, exact verb table, second-by-second mechanics.
     Graph note:
       This is not a vertical ladder rung but a cross-cutting parent/gate. It should read q-full-game-descent-arc and the
       frozen gas/city parents before q-expedition-macrocycle is resumed.

  4. q-expedition-macrocycle — RENAME/SPLIT FROM q-expedition-loop
     Core question:
       What is one expedition from entry to saved return or failure?
     Why it must precede floor loop:
       A floor loop needs boundaries: how the team enters, what a floor transition means, when deeper/out decisions happen,
       what carries forward, and when results become safe.
     Decides:
       Expedition phases, surface/lift/shaft role, commit points, multi-floor structure, extraction/failure boundary, what
       state travels between floors.
     Must not decide:
       Inside-floor verbs, exact gas tasks, room jobs, route-making details, economy numbers, UI.

  5. q-floor-loop — ADD
     Core question:
       After the lift/entry opens onto one floor, what does the team do before it can continue deeper, extract, or fail?
     Why it must precede concrete mechanics:
       Concrete mechanics need a home: what floor job they serve and how they hand back into expedition state.
     Decides:
       Floor phases, room/job slots, required reads, split/together rhythm, objective handoff, local success/failure output.
     Must not decide:
       The exact worked loop of each mechanic, final gas reactions, balance, full content taxonomy.

  6. concrete mechanic questions — DEFER UNTIL ABOVE PARENTS EXIST
     Core question:
       What are the worked playable loops in units/seconds with separated players?
     Why they come last:
       Mechanic-forge requires concrete verbs and consequences; before parent loops, it will fill gaps with plausible but
       non-canon placeholders.
     Decides:
       Verb table, objects, inputs, perceivable consequences, 2+ player requirement, second-by-second scenario, lens status.
     Must not decide:
       Global arc, between-run progression, depth grammar, expedition boundary, floor structure.
