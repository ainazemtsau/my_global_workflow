  REPLACEMENT CRITERIA

  - No alternatives before a reversible readiness check.
  - Every relevant DESIGN ANCHOR is visible and has explicit treatment without becoming a
    prerequisite, evidence, a guaranteed option, a winner or canon.
  - Alternatives are structurally distinct and compared in the same scene/fixture with criteria
    fixed before rendering.
  - The owner sees one compact decision surface, not the backend graph or an essay.
  - Paper, visual, prototype and human-playtest claims use different evidence lanes and explicitly
    state what they do not test.
  - Canon promotion is a downstream local recommendation after question-appropriate maturity, not
    a universal external fact and not a claim that every canon statement needs a prototype.
  - Side ideas carry provenance, linked question and wake condition without changing the active
    bundle.

  STRUCTURALLY DIFFERENT ARCHITECTURES

  A. Readiness-gated bundle -> evidence ladder -> separate Canon Ledger (recommended backbone).
     Mechanism: one ready bundle, matched alternatives, owner/evidence outcome, later canon review.
     Strength: lowest operating cost and direct coverage of all five failures. Cost: low-medium.
     Failure modes: false readiness, serial fixation, gate bureaucracy. Strongest refutation:
     game-design problems and solutions co-evolve; a hard pre-ideation gate can become the same
     linear queue with more ceremony. Required countermeasure: minimal/reversible readiness,
     justified co-framing and traceable return-on-gap.

  B. Set-based scene portfolio. Mechanism: keep 2-3 option families alive and narrow only when the
     same fixture/evidence discriminates them. Strength: resists premature point commitment and
     handles coupled questions. Cost: medium-high. Failure modes: option overload, prose catalogue,
     delayed decision and expensive equal development. Strongest refutation: Toyota-style evidence
     transfers poorly to one owner/solo developer; carrying alternatives can cost more than it
     learns. Kill after a round that eliminates/changes no option and yields no discriminating probe.

  C. Experiment-led hypothesis portfolio. Mechanism: start from a falsifiable prediction, choose
     the cheapest valid paper/visual/prototype/playtest fixture, observe a predeclared signal, then
     retain/revise/refute. Strength: strongest guard against paper-clean = fun. Cost: medium-high in
     build/test time. Failure modes: executable assumptions, local overfitting, metric theatre and
     endless testing. Strongest refutation: semantic/value decisions may not be empirically decidable;
     prototype-first on undefined entities merely freezes an accident in code.

  RECOMMENDATION AND CHANGE CONDITIONS

  Use A as the spine; borrow B only for a cheap micro-set inside a justified coupled bundle; route
  to C only when the claim is experiential. Change the recommendation if the pilot shows any of:
  (a) readiness cannot usefully be judged before limited generation; (b) matched scenes do not expose
  important differences; (c) maintaining alternatives costs more than the uncertainty warrants;
  (d) the owner needs backend process explanation to use the one-page surface.

  STATUS MODEL (three orthogonal primitive axes; named statuses are composites)

  - authority: NONE | FOUNDATION | DESIGN_ANCHOR | CANON.
  - evidence_mode: PAPER | VISUAL | PROTOTYPE | PLAYTEST.
  - evidence_disposition: HYPOTHESIS | SUPPORTED | REFUTED | NOT_RUN.
  - workflow: CAPTURED | BLOCKED | READY | ACTIVE | HOLD | OWNER_DECIDED | REJECTED | SUPERSEDED.

  PAPER_DECISION = authority NONE + evidence PAPER + workflow OWNER_DECIDED: a reversible working
  direction or paper-scope decision; it cannot claim visual legibility, runtime behaviour or fun.
  VISUAL_HYPOTHESIS and PROTOTYPE_HYPOTHESIS name their exact claim/falsifier and untested dimensions.
  PLAYTEST_SUPPORTED_DESIGN is evidence, still not canon. FROZEN_CANON = authority CANON, created
  only by a separate owner-verbatim promotion after the question's declared maturity gate.

  DESIGN_ANCHOR precisely means owner-liked directional material with exact quote/source/date,
  scope and linked questions. Every intersecting bundle shows treatment: preserved or challenged.
  Silence preserves the anchor but does not make it a constraint. Revision/rejection/supersession
  requires an explicit transition naming old form, proposed change, reason/evidence and owner words;
  the old form remains retrievable.

  DEPENDENCY / READINESS REPRESENTATION

  Internal decision graph node: decision; not_deciding; terms/entities; required_outputs; anchor
  links/treatment; criteria; evidence mode; matched fixture; falsifier; status; next evidence.
  Each edge carries type, rationale, condition/predicate, provenance, output_needed and
  minimum_maturity:
  - requires_all: every conditional prerequisite must supply its named output/maturity;
  - coframe_with: symmetric and legal only with a named shared unknown, one shared discriminating
    probe and an exit rule;
  - informs: non-blocking evidence;
  - invalidates_if: observable evidence predicate that reopens the node.
  A cycle becomes one coupled bundle only if the co-frame rule holds; otherwise it BLOCKS and opens
  the cheapest discovery probe. READY additionally requires defined entities, predeclared criteria,
  fixture, evidence mode, anchor trace and possible next evidence. Pilot parameter: one active bundle
  and at most two visible ready bundles. A hidden prerequisite stops generation, records the edge,
  marks affected candidates HOLD/invalidated-by-gap, recomputes the front, and returns to the same
  bundle after evidence; it never silently takes the next queue item.

  COMPACT OWNER SURFACE

  One screen: decision / not-decision; readiness strip; relevant anchors + treatment; one matched
  fixture; 2-3 equal cards; criteria; recommendation + strongest refutation after the cards; owner
  outcome; captures (source + wake). For mechanic/gameplay bundles each card uses the same scene and
  rows: player intention -> input/action -> simulation change -> perceivable consequence -> tradeoff/
  failure/recovery -> evidence gap/falsifier. Visual/lore/tone questions use matched frames/vignettes,
  not a forced 20-second mechanic scene. The compactness benefit is a pilot hypothesis, not an
  established guarantee.

  EXACT PILOT BRIEF

  Purpose: test the process, not answer/freeze core-0 gas-state semantics or core-1 Bubble filling.
  Inputs: three foundations; Bubble/three phases/sleeping-gas recon as nonbinding DESIGN ANCHORS;
  core-0/core-1 as HOLD source; INDEX/canon unchanged.
  Process hypothesis: the process either justifies requires/co-frame/readiness before candidates or
  stops on a named prerequisite; when it reaches comparison, the owner can explain and compare
  mechanically distinct hypotheses in one fixture and name the next evidence without canon pressure.
  Pilot question: "Is the core-0 <-> core-1 bundle ready for alternative comparison, and what evidence
  mode is required next?"

  Stage R: owner teach-back of decision/non-decision; classify the relationship using edge fields;
  enumerate undefined entities; choose evidence mode before candidates. BLOCKED with exact return
  evidence is a readiness HALF-PASS only: pause the same pilot, satisfy the prerequisite, then resume;
  no full process pass is declared until comparison and downstream separation are exercised.

  Stage C (only after READY or justified co-frame): during the future pilot, not this research,
  create 2-3 disposable mechanically distinct hypotheses. Use one labelled pilot fixture: two players,
  one room+doorway, same starting positions and 0-20s window, a source and empty containment object.
  Any state profiles used as controlled variables must be explicitly defined for the fixture and marked
  non-canon; no candidate may rely on an undefined S. Render identical rows and ask only for the process
  verdict: understandable? comparable? enough evidence/what next? A provisional game preference is
  pilot data, not a design/canon verdict. Captures get provenance+wake; canon diff remains empty.

  PILOT PASS / KILL

  Pass only when the owner explains decision/non-decision in two sentences; dependency/co-frame is
  evidenced; no candidate precedes readiness or uses undefined entities; at least two candidates differ
  in action->system->consequence; fixture/rows/criteria are equal; evidence mode and untested dimensions
  are explicit; an anchor is visible/nonbinding; captures are retrievable; no canon is written; and a
  primary next evidence requirement is named. A readiness half-pass must later complete Stage C.

  Automatic fail: owner cannot explain the decision; undefined entity; prose variants; unequal scenes/
  criteria/detail; verdict pressure before sufficient evidence; paper/visual/prototype evidence claims
  beyond its lane; anchor lost or silently hardened; side idea hijacks or lacks provenance/wake; hidden
  prerequisite falls through to another question; no next evidence; canon/INDEX mutation; or set-based
  round produces neither learning nor a discriminating probe.
