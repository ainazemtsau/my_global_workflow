# Primary Product Bet — Expedition

Status:
Authoritative durable game-truth

Date created:
2026-04-25

Source Goal:
Выбрать одну primary product bet через evaluation frame и explicit experience model

## Selected primary bet

Expedition / session-structured living reactive gas sandbox

## Rejected alternative

Containment / crisis ops

Containment remains a valid alternative, but it is rejected as the primary product bet for the current game direction.

Containment is clearer and easier to explain: players respond to a crisis, localize danger, seal routes, stabilize the situation and restore control. Its problem is that this frame creates a higher burden for authored or generated incident solvability. If the generated incident is bad, the player is likely to read it as a broken objective, unfair puzzle or failed scenario.

Expedition is selected because dangerous, uneven or unfavorable generated spaces can still make sense inside an expedition frame. A team can retreat, abandon the level, choose another depth, try another route or attempt another extraction. Failure does not have to mean that the design failed; it can mean that the expedition was too risky, badly handled or not worth continuing.

Containment-like actions may appear locally inside Expedition — sealing a door, stabilizing a room, stopping spread, isolating a hazard — but only as tactics that preserve route viability, cargo viability or extraction options. They are not the product identity and not the main success condition.

The selected identity is not:

“restore control over an incident.”

The selected identity is:

“make an expedition judgment inside a living gas/topology/cargo system.”

## Stable product identity

A 2–4 player co-op expedition game where the team chooses a dangerous procedural gas environment, enters it to extract value, manipulates gas and topology to create temporary safety, handles unstable carried loot, and decides whether to push deeper, reroute, retreat or abandon the run.

A compatible fiction frame is:

players return to a remote planet, underground industrial / pharmacological station, old quarantine site or similar abandoned dangerous environment to extract value from spaces now dominated by living, semi-living, reactive or mutating gas behavior.

This fiction is usable framing material, not locked lore.

## Why Expedition won

Expedition won because it better preserves and amplifies the project’s most distinctive product promise:

a co-op team moving through, manipulating and surviving a living gas environment whose topology-mediated consequences persist beyond the team’s immediate presence.

The decisive reasons:

1. Gas identity is more distinctive in Expedition

In Containment, gas is a strong incident system.

In Expedition, gas becomes the living medium of the whole run:

- hazard;
- tool;
- route-shaper;
- consequence source;
- story generator;
- cargo risk.

2. Topology is more product-defining in Expedition

In Containment, topology is an operational control surface.

In Expedition, topology is the space of traversal, opportunity, risk, retreat, overcommitment and cascading consequence.

3. Procgen has a stronger experiential reason in Expedition

Containment uses procgen for incident variation.

Expedition uses procgen as unknown living route pressure: players do not know which routes, pockets, bottlenecks, shafts and safe windows will shape this run.

4. Expedition better tolerates procedural unevenness

In Expedition, a difficult, hostile or unfavorable generated space can still be valid as part of expedition risk.

The team can:

- retreat;
- cut losses;
- choose another level;
- try another route;
- push a different depth;
- come back later with another plan.

In Containment, a badly generated incident more easily reads as a broken or unfair objective, because the frame promises a crisis that can be diagnosed and operationally resolved.

5. Co-op harm fits Expedition as emergent systemic consequence

Expedition creates self-authored consequence:

- a teammate opens the wrong door;
- delays retreat;
- seals the wrong path;
- releases gas into a future return route;
- mishandles unstable gas-bearing loot;
- creates a cascade that later transforms extraction.

## Locked session / run structure

Bounded co-op expedition run with pre-run target choice and in-run push / reroute / retreat / abandon decisions.

The session is not one linear handcrafted path.

Before or at the beginning of a run, the team chooses a target, level, depth, route family, site or risk band. That choice defines the kind of danger and value opportunity they are willing to enter.

## Locked minimum action contour

Choose expedition risk → enter → read gas/topology/value → alter route or flow through spatial decisions → create temporary safety or access → recover unstable value → coordinate carrying or securing → manage readable gas/cargo consequence → push / reroute / secure / retreat / abandon.

This is a shape-level contour, not a tool roster.

## Locked procgen function

Procedural generation player-facing function:

unknown living route pressure

Procgen is not included because it is generally good, cheaper to produce or merely replayable. Its function is to make each expedition a risk assessment problem around unknown topology, gas state, value location, unstable cargo and return-path viability.

## Locked reaction grammar status

Reaction grammar status:

shape-level truth

Concrete reaction recipes status:

hypothesis material, not hard commitments

Gas types and reactions matter because they make the environment feel alive and worth expedition-style play. But this note does not lock specific substances, tiers, recipes, balancing, reaction tables, formulas, rupture rules, explosion rules or knockback rules.

## Locked harm / friendly-fire status

Core as indirect systemic and cargo-mediated co-op harm.

Severity is an optional intensity axis.

Opaque direct grief damage is a non-goal.

The target is not arbitrary PvP or trolling. The target is:

Your action changed the environment or cargo state in a way that put me at risk, and we can understand why.

## Locked co-op pattern

Shared environmental and cargo improvisation under route commitment.

Players are not doing parallel solo tasks. They share one gas/topology/cargo state.

One player’s action can:

- make another route safer;
- contaminate another route;
- create or remove temporary safety;
- release gas from carried loot;
- cause another player to drop cargo;
- force the team to change extraction plans.

## Player / value promise

A 2–4 player team enters dangerous procedural gas spaces to extract value, survives by reading and manipulating a living environment, handles unstable gas-bearing loot, and remembers runs through route choices, temporary safety, gas reactions, cargo mistakes, overcommitment, retreat and extraction.

The promise is not:

Solve a fixed mission.

The promise is:

Make an expedition judgment inside a living gas/topology/cargo system.

## Anti-audience

This bet is not for players who primarily want:

- pure combat where the environment is secondary;
- clean authored puzzle solutions with guaranteed solvability;
- low-friction cozy co-op without teammate-caused danger;
- deterministic mission scripting where every run has a designed answer;
- solo-first play where co-op is optional decoration;
- fixed role/class execution more than environmental improvisation;
- progression grind as the main reason to play;
- total clarity at all times with no uncertainty, retreat or loss;
- chaotic physics comedy where causality does not matter;
- hardcore scientific simulation where physical correctness matters more than readable game consequence;
- frictionless loot pickup where extraction cargo is never dangerous;
- co-op without communication, timing or mutual dependence.

The intended audience must tolerate uncertainty, environmental risk, partial information, unstable cargo, imperfect team decisions and the possibility that the correct expedition decision is to leave, reroute or abandon value.
